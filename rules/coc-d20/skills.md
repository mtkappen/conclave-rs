# Chapter 2: Skills

Your skills represent a variety of abilities, and you get better at them as you go up in level.

## How Skills Work

### Acquiring Skill Ranks

Ranks indicate how much training or experience your character has with a given skill. Each of your skills has a rank, from 0 (for a skill in which your character has no training at all) to 23 (for the 20th-level character who has spent every single skill rank on that one skill).

**Core Skills**: Skills found in your character's profession template cost 1 skill point per rank. Maximum rank = level + 3

**Noncore Skills**: All other skills cost 2 skill points per rank (you get a half-rank per skill point). Maximum rank = half of core skill maximum

### Acquiring Skills at 1st Level

1. **Determine skill points**: (8 + Intelligence modifier) × 4
2. **Spend the skill points**: Each skill point on a core skill gets you 1 rank; each skill point on a noncore skill gets you a half-rank
3. **Max out skills**: You can max out nine core skills at 4 ranks each with 36 points

### Skills at Higher Levels

When your character reaches a new experience level:

1. Determine the number of skill points you get: 8 + Intelligence modifier (minimum 1)
2. Improve any previously maxed-out core skill by 1 rank, or noncore skill by a half-rank
3. If you haven't maxed out a skill, spend extra skill points to increase its rank further
4. Pick up new skills: Spend up to your level + 3 skill points on it (1 rank each for core, half-rank each for noncore)

## Skill Checks

A skill check takes into account your training (skill ranks), natural talent (ability modifier), and luck (the die roll). It may also take into account other factors.

**To make a skill check**: Roll 1d20 and add your skill modifier for that skill. The higher the result, the better you do.

### Skill Modifier Formula
```
Skill Modifier = Skill Ranks + Ability Modifier + Miscellaneous Modifiers
```

### Difficulty Class (DC)

Some checks are made against a Difficulty Class (DC), set by the Gamemaster:

| Difficulty | DC  | Example                          |
|------------|-----|----------------------------------|
| Very Easy  | 0   | Notice something in plain sight  |
| Easy       | 5   | Climb a knotted rope             |
| Average    | 10  | Hear a security guard approaching|
| Tough      | 15  | Disarm an explosive device       |
| Challenging| 20  | Track an escaped madman in rain  |
| Formidable | 25  | Break into a secure computer system |
| Heroic     | 30  | Leap across a 30-foot chasm      |

### Opposed Checks

Some skill checks are opposed checks, made against another character's skill check result:

| Task                      | Skill (Key Ability)    | Opposing Skill (Key Ability) |
|---------------------------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Sneak up behind someone   | Move Silently (Dex)    | Listen (Wis)                 |
| Con someone               | Bluff (Cha)            | Sense Motive (Wis)           |
| Hide from someone         | Hide (Dex)             | Spot (Wis)                   |
| Tie a prisoner securely   | Use Rope (Dex)         | Escape Artist (Dex)          |
| Win a horserace           | Ride (Dex)             | Ride (Dex)                   |
| Pass as someone else      | Disguise (Cha)         | Spot (Wis)                   |
| Steal a wallet            | Sleight of Hand (Dex)  | Spot (Wis)                   |
| Create a false ID         | Forgery (Int)          | Forgery (Int)                |

For ties on opposed checks, the character with the higher key ability score wins. If these scores are the same, flip a coin.

### Untrained Skill Checks

Most skills can be used even if the character has no ranks in them—this is known as using an untrained skill or making an untrained skill check. A few skills can only be used if you have ranks in them; these are called **trained skills**.

When you use a skill with no ranks, you don't add any ranks to your skill check, but you do add your ability modifier for that skill's key ability.

### Retries

In general, you can try a skill check again if you fail, and can keep trying indefinitely. Some skills, however, have consequences of failure that must be taken into account:

- **Open Lock**: Can retry, but alarm may go off if missed by 5 or more
- **Climb**: Can retry, but falling is a penalty if failed by 5 or more
- **Heal**: If check fails on dying character, they probably lose another hit point

### Favorable and Unfavorable Conditions

Some situations may make a skill easier or harder to use. This might add a bonus or penalty to a skill check or change the DC:

1. Give the skill user a +2 circumstance bonus
2. Reduce (or increase) the DC by 2
3. Both (+2 modifier and -2 DC = +4 effective bonus)

### Time and Skill Checks

When time is a factor, the time it takes to use a skill is measured in rounds (each round is about 6 seconds). Some skills require specific types of actions: move action, attack action, or full-round action.

#### Taking 10

When you are not in a rush and not being threatened or distracted, you may choose to take 10. Instead of rolling 1d20 for the skill check, calculate your result as if you had rolled a 10, then add your skill modifier. For many routine tasks, taking 10 makes them automatically successful.

**Distractions or threats make it impossible to take 10.**

#### Taking 20

Taking 20 takes twenty times as long as making a single check would take. When you have plenty of time (generally 2 minutes for a skill that can normally be used in 1 round), and when the skill being attempted carries no penalties for failure, you can take 20. In other words, eventually you will get a 20 if you roll long enough.

### Cooperation

Sometimes characters can work together and help each other out. One character is considered the leader of the effort and makes a skill check; each helper makes a skill check against DC 10. For each helper who succeeds, the leader gets a +2 circumstance bonus.

**Note**: Usually only one helper can assist at a time (a third person would just get in the way).

### Skill Synergy

It's possible for a character to have two skills that always work well together. Having 5 or more ranks in one skill gives you a +2 synergy bonus on skill checks with its related skill(s):

| Five Ranks In           | Grants +2 Bonus To        |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| Animal Empathy          | Handle Animal             |
| Bluff                   | Intimidate, Sleight of Hand |
| Handle Animal           | Ride                      |
| Knowledge (medicine)    | Heal                      |
| Knowledge (psychology)  | Psychoanalysis            |
| Sense Motive            | Diplomacy                 |
| Tumble                  | Balance, Jump             |

### Bonus Types

Modifiers with specific designators generally do not stack with others of the same type—only the best bonus or worst penalty of that type applies. Exceptions: dodge bonuses and circumstance bonuses stack with each other.

Examples of bonus types: aptitude, armor, circumstance, competence, deflection, dodge, enhancement, insight, luck, morale, natural armor, synergy.

## Skill Descriptions

### Animal Empathy {Cha}
**Description**: This skill allows you to understand and empathize with an animal's feelings. You can use this skill to discern an animal's state of mind, or change its attitude toward you.

**Check**: Use this skill to detect an animal's hostility or friendliness. A successful check against DC 15 (or higher) lets you know if the animal is likely to attack. You can also use it to improve an animal's attitude.

### Appraise {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to estimate the value of commonplace items, including art objects, gems, merchandise, and weapons.

**Check**: To appraise an item, make an Appraise check against a DC determined by the GM. Common items are DC 10; rare or unusual items have higher DCs. You get a +2 circumstance bonus on Appraise checks involving any item that is small or highly detailed if you use a scale.

### Balance {Dex}
**Description**: This skill lets you walk across a narrow or slippery surface, or stay upright when knocked off balance.

**Check**: The DC depends on the width and condition of the surface:
- 2 to 4 inches wide: DC 10
- Less than 2 inches wide: DC 15+
- Slippery (oil, ice): +5 DC
- Uneven (rubble, roots): +5 DC

If you fail by 5 or more, you fall.

### Bluff {Cha}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to lie or mislead others.

**Check**: You can use Bluff to fool someone with a falsehood. The DC depends on how believable the lie is and how much evidence the target has to the contrary. A successful opposed Bluff check (vs. Sense Motive) means you fool the target.

### Climb {Str}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to scale sheer surfaces.

**Check**: Make a Climb check to climb a surface. The DC depends on the surface:
- Slope less than 60°: DC 0
- Wall less than 60°: DC 15
- Vertical wall or overhang: DC 20+

If you fail by 5 or more, you fall from your current height. You can take 10 on a Climb check when not threatened.

### Computer Use {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to use computers and computerized devices.

**Check**: You can use this skill to hack into computer systems, bypass security measures, or recover data. DCs vary based on system complexity and security.

### Concentration {Con}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to focus despite distractions.

**Check**: Use this skill when you must concentrate on something while under duress (such as taking damage, casting a spell while moving, etc.). DC depends on the level of distraction.

### Craft {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to create items in various crafts. Each specialization is a separate skill.

**Specializations**: Alchemy, Blacksmithing, Carpentry, Cooking, Glassblowing, Leatherworking, Painting, Pottery, Sculpture, Shipbuilding, Smithing, Weaving, etc.

**Check**: Make a Craft check to create an item. The DC depends on the complexity of the item. Success means you complete the item in the appropriate time.

### Demolitions {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to set and detonate explosives safely and effectively.

**Check**: You can use this skill to defuse explosive devices (DC 15+), set charges for maximum effect, or create improvised explosives.

### Diplomacy {Cha}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to influence and negotiate with others.

**Check**: Use Diplomacy to change NPC attitudes. The DC depends on the initial attitude and desired outcome:
- Hostile to Indifferent: DC 20
- Unfriendly to Friendly: DC 15
- Indifferent to Helpful: DC 10

### Disable Device {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to tamper with mechanical devices, including locks, traps, and bombs.

**Check**: You can use this skill to disable traps (DC varies), jam mechanisms, or sabotage equipment. Trained only.

### Disguise {Cha}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to alter your appearance.

**Check**: Make a Disguise check when attempting to impersonate someone or hide your identity. The DC depends on how convincing the disguise needs to be and whether observers have reason to suspect you.

### Drive {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to operate motor vehicles.

**Check**: Use this skill to control vehicles in difficult situations, perform stunts, or avoid accidents. Different vehicle types may require different checks.

### Escape Artist {Dex}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to slip out of bonds and tight spaces.

**Check**: You can use Escape Artist to escape from ropes, handcuffs, grapples, or tight spaces. DC depends on how well you're bound or the size of the space.

### Forgery {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to create fake documents, signatures, and other written materials.

**Check**: Make a Forgery check to create a counterfeit document. The DC depends on the complexity of the forgery and whether someone is comparing it to an original.

### Gather Information {Cha}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to collect information from NPCs through conversation.

**Check**: Spend 1d4 hours asking around and make a Gather Information check. The DC depends on how well-guarded the information is and how cooperative the locals are.

### Heal {Wis}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to treat injuries and diseases.

**Checks**:
- **Treat Injury**: Stabilize a dying character (DC 15), treat wounds, or provide long-term care
- **Diagnose Disease**: Identify a disease (DC 15)
- **Treat Poison**: Provide first aid for poison (DC 20)

### Hide {Dex}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to conceal yourself from others.

**Check**: Make a Hide check opposed by observers' Spot checks. You need cover or concealment to hide effectively. Armor check penalty applies.

### Innuendo {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to pick up on hints, implications, and hidden meanings in conversation.

**Check**: Use Innuendo to detect when someone is being evasive, hinting at something, or speaking in code. The GM sets the DC based on how subtle the communication is.

### Intimidate {Cha}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to frighten others into cooperation.

**Check**: Make an Intimidate check opposed by the target's Sense Motive check. Success means you can make the target unfriendly or fearful.

### Knowledge {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your knowledge of various academic and specialized fields. Each specialization is a separate skill.

**Specializations**: Archaeology, Art, Biology, Engineering, Geography, History, Local, Medicine, Occult, Philosophy, Physics, Religion, Science, etc.

**Check**: Make a Knowledge check to recall information about a subject. DCs vary based on how obscure the information is:
- Common knowledge: DC 10
- Uncommon but available: DC 15
- Obscure: DC 20+
- Extremely obscure: DC 25+

### Listen {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to hear sounds and detect noises.

**Checks**:
- **Hear a sound**: DC varies by distance and volume
- **Listen for trouble**: DC 10 to notice unusual activity
- **Overhear conversation**: DC depends on distance and clarity

### Move Silently {Dex}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to move without making noise.

**Check**: Make a Move Silently check opposed by observers' Listen checks. Armor check penalty applies.

### Open Lock {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to pick locks and bypass mechanical security.

**Check**: Make an Open Lock check against the lock's DC. Simple locks are DC 20; complex or magical locks have higher DCs.

### Operate Heavy Machinery {Dex}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to operate large, complex machines.

**Check**: Use this skill to operate cranes, industrial equipment, heavy vehicles, and other machinery.

### Performance {Cha}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to entertain others. Each specialization is a separate skill.

**Specializations**: Acting, Comedy, Dance, Instrument (various), Singing, Storytelling, etc.

**Check**: Make a Performance check to entertain an audience. The DC depends on the audience's sophistication and expectations.

### Pilot {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to operate aircraft and watercraft.

**Check**: Use this skill to fly or pilot vehicles in difficult situations, perform maneuvers, or avoid collisions.

### Psychic Focus {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your mental discipline and ability to focus psychic abilities.

**Check**: Use this skill when using psychic feats or abilities that require concentration.

### Psychoanalysis {Wis}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to analyze and treat mental conditions.

**Check**: You can use this skill to diagnose mental disorders, provide therapy, or help someone recover from insanity.

### Read Lips {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to understand speech by watching lip movements.

**Check**: Make a Read Lips check to understand someone speaking without hearing them. DC depends on distance, lighting, and how clearly you can see the speaker's lips.

### Repair {Int}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to fix broken mechanical devices.

**Check**: Make a Repair check to fix damaged equipment. DC varies based on the extent of damage and complexity of the item.

### Research {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to find information in libraries, archives, and other sources.

**Check**: Spend time researching and make a Research check. The DC depends on how obscure the information is and how well-organized your sources are.

### Ride {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to ride animals, particularly horses.

**Checks**: You can use this skill to guide your mount with your knees, perform stunts, or stay mounted when threatened. Armor check penalty applies.

### Search {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to find hidden or concealed items.

**Check**: Make a Search check to find secret doors, traps, or hidden objects. Each 5-foot square takes a full-round action to search thoroughly.

### Sense Motive {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to understand others' motivations and detect deception.

**Checks**:
- **Hunch**: Get a general sense of someone's intentions (DC 20)
- **Forewarned**: Gain a bonus on initiative if you succeed (DC 25)
- **Detect Bluff**: Opposed check vs. target's Bluff

### Sleight of Hand {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to perform tricks that require manual dexterity and misdirection.

**Checks**: You can use this skill to pick pockets, conceal objects, or perform magic tricks. DC varies based on the complexity of the trick and observer vigilance.

### Speak Other Language {Int}
**Description**: This skill represents your knowledge of languages other than English (or your native tongue).

**Check**: Each language is a separate skill purchase. You must have at least 1 rank to speak the language fluently.

### Spot {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to notice details and detect things that aren't obvious.

**Checks**:
- **Notice something**: DC varies based on what you're looking for
- **Spot a hidden creature**: Opposed by target's Hide check
- **Detect a secret door**: DC 20+

### Swim {Str}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to swim and stay afloat.

**Check**: Make a Swim check to swim through water. DC depends on conditions:
- Calm water: DC 10
- Rough water: DC 15
- Stormy water: DC 20

If you fail by 5 or more, you go underwater. Armor check penalty applies.

### Tumble {Dex}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to move acrobatically and avoid attacks.

**Checks**: You can use this skill to dodge attacks, perform acrobatic maneuvers, or move through threatened squares without provoking attacks of opportunity. Armor check penalty applies.

### Use Rope {Dex}
**Description**: This skill covers your ability to tie knots and use ropes effectively.

**Checks**: You can use this skill to tie complex knots, bind prisoners, create rope traps, or climb using a rope.

### Wilderness Lore {Wis}
**Description**: This skill represents your ability to navigate and survive in the wilderness.

**Checks**:
- **Find food/water**: DC 15+ depending on terrain
- **Track**: DC varies based on conditions
- **Navigate**: DC 10 to avoid getting lost
- **Predict weather**: DC 15
