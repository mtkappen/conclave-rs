# Magic Items

Magic items are wondrous creations that can grant characters extraordinary powers. This section covers the general rules for using magic items.

## Magic Item Categories

### Armor
Armor with magical properties, such as enhanced protection or special abilities:
- **Examples:** Armor of Resistance, Plate Mail +1, Shield +2
- **Attunement:** Some armor requires attunement

### Weapons
Weapons that deal extra damage or have special properties:
- **Examples:** Longsword +1, Dagger of Venom, Holy Avenger
- **Attunement:** Many magical weapons require attunement

### Potions
Liquid magic that produces effects when consumed:
- **Examples:** Potion of Healing, Potion of Flying, Potion of Giant Strength
- **Usage:** Typically a Standard Action to drink

### Rings
Magical rings worn on fingers:
- **Examples:** Ring of Protection, Ring of Spell Storing, Ring of Invisibility
- **Attunement:** Most magical rings require attunement

### Rods, Staves, and Wands
Focus items for spellcasters or sources of magical power:
- **Rods:** Powerful magical implements (Rod of Lordly Might)
- **Staves:** Contain multiple spells (Staff of Power)
- **Wands:** Store specific spells (Wand of Fireballs)

### Wondrous Items
Miscellaneous magical items that don't fit other categories:
- **Examples:** Bag of Holding, Cloak of Invisibility, Crystal Ball
- **Variety:** Extremely diverse in function and power

## Magic Item Rarity

Rarity indicates how powerful and uncommon a magic item is:

### Common (1–50 gp value equivalent)
- Minor magical effects
- Usually don't require attunement
- Examples: Oil of Slipperiness, Potion of Climbing

### Uncommon (51–500 gp value equivalent)
- Moderate magical effects
- Some require attunement
- Examples: +1 weapons, Cloak of Protection, Boots of Striding and Springing

### Rare (501–5,000 gp value equivalent)
- Significant magical powers
- Often require attunement
- Examples: +2 weapons, Ring of Spell Storing, Staff of the Magi

### Very Rare (5,001–50,000 gp value equivalent)
- Powerful magical effects
- Almost always require attunement
- Examples: +3 weapons, Holy Avenger, Deck of Many Things

### Legendary (50,001+ gp value equivalent)
- Extraordinary powers
- Always require attunement
- Examples: Excalibur, Orb of Dragonkind, Artifact items

## Attunement

Some magic items require a creature to form a bond called attunement before they can be used effectively.

### Requirements
- You must have the required proficiency or class feature
- You must spend a Short Rest focusing on the item
- You can't attune to more than three items at once
- Attunement ends if you no longer meet requirements, die, or are 5 feet away for 24 hours

### Breaking Attunement
You can end attunement by:
- Spending a Short Rest focusing on the item (unless it's cursed)
- Having the DM break the bond due to circumstances
- Dying (attunement ends immediately)

## Activating a Magic Item

### Spell Activation
Some items allow you to cast spells from them:
- **Spell Slots:** Some items use your spell slots
- **Item Charges:** Others have their own charges
- **Casting Time:** Follows the spell's normal casting time unless stated otherwise

### Command Word
Speaking a command word activates certain items:
- Requires an action to speak the word
- The word is often in a specific language (usually the item's creator's language)
- Some items have hidden command words that must be discovered

### Charges
Some items have charges that are expended when used:
- **Regaining Charges:** Some items regain charges at dawn or other times
- **Running Out:** Items may become nonmagical or break when charges reach 0
- **Risk:** Using the last charge might require a saving throw to avoid destruction

## Cursed Items

Some magic items are cursed, causing harm or hindrance to those who use them:

### Identifying Curses
- **Detection:** Detect Magic reveals magical aura but not curse
- **Identification:** Identify spell doesn't reveal curses
- **Discovery:** Usually discovered when the item is used

### Common Curse Effects
- **Bonded Items:** Can't be removed or attuned to other items
- **Penalties:** Disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, or saving throws
- **Compulsions:** Forced actions or behaviors
- **Transformation:** Physical changes to the character

### Removing Curses
- **Remove Curse:** Standard spell for removing most curses
- **Greater Restoration:** For powerful or complex curses
- **DM Discretion:** Some curses require special quests or conditions

## Magic Item Resilience

Magic items have different durability than normal items:

### Damage and Destruction
- **Resistance:** Most magic items have Resistance to all damage
- **Immunity:** Some legendary items are immune to all damage
- **Saving Throws:** Magic items make saving throws as if they were creatures

### Specific Rules
- **Armor/Weapons:** Can be damaged like normal armor/weapons unless specified
- **Wondrous Items:** Often have specific rules for destruction
- **Artifacts:** Nearly indestructible; require special methods to destroy

## Crafting Magic Items

Creating magic items requires time, gold, and often special knowledge:

### Requirements
- **Spellcasting:** Ability to cast the spells required (or access to them)
- **Formula/Recipe:** Knowledge of how to create the item
- **Materials:** Rare components specific to the item
- **Time:** Days or weeks depending on rarity

### Process
1. **Research:** Learn the formula for the item
2. **Gather Materials:** Acquire rare components and gold
3. **Crafting:** Spend time creating the item (typically 8 hours per 100 gp)
4. **Testing:** May require ability checks or saving throws

### Costs by Rarity
- **Common:** 50–100 gp, 1–5 days
- **Uncommon:** 200–500 gp, 5–30 days
- **Rare:** 2,000–5,000 gp, 30–90 days
- **Very Rare:** 20,000–50,000 gp, 90–180 days
- **Legendary:** 100,000+ gp, 180+ days

## Finding Magic Items

Magic items are treasures to be discovered:

### Distribution
- **Campaign Style:** High-magic campaigns have more items
- **DM Discretion:** DMs decide what items appear in their world
- **Story Integration:** Items should fit the campaign narrative

### Acquisition Methods
- **Treasure Hoards:** Found with monsters or in dungeons
- **Quest Rewards:** Given for completing important tasks
- **Purchase:** Available from merchants (rare and expensive)
- **Crafting:** Created by player characters
