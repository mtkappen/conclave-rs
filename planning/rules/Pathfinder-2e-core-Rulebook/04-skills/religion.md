# Religion (Wisdom)

Religion measures your knowledge of divine agents, divine planes, theology, obscure myths, and creatures of religious significance.

## Untrained Actions

### Recall Knowledge
Recall Knowledge about:
- Divine agents (deities, angels, devils, etc.)
- Divine planes (Heaven, Hell, Elysium, etc.)
- Theology and religious doctrines
- Obscure myths and legends
- Creatures of religious significance

## Trained Actions

### Decipher Writing - EXPLORATION SECRET
When you encounter particularly archaic or esoteric texts, the GM might require you to Decipher the Writing before you can understand it. You must be trained in Religion to decipher religious scriptures or theological texts. This usually takes 1 minute per page of text, but might take longer for ancient or coded religious writings.

- **Critical Success:** You understand the true meaning of the text, including any hidden spiritual or theological messages.
- **Success:** You understand the true meaning of the text. If it was a coded document, you know the general meaning but might not have a word-for-word translation.
- **Failure:** You can't understand the text and take a –2 circumstance penalty to further checks to decipher it.
- **Critical Failure:** You believe you understand the text on that page, but you have in fact misconstrued its message.

### Identify Magic - EXPLORATION SECRET
Using Religion, you can attempt to identify divine magic, including divine spells, magical items with the divine trait, and creatures with divine abilities. This works like the general Identify Magic action but specifically applies to divine traditions.

- **Critical Success:** You learn all attributes of the divine magic, including its name, effects, and any means of activating it.
- **Success:** For an item or location, you get a sense of what it does and learn any means of activating it. For an ongoing effect, you learn the effect's name and what it does.
- **Failure:** You fail to identify the magic and can't try again for 1 day.
- **Critical Failure:** You misidentify the magic as something else.

### Learn a Spell - EXPLORATION
If you're a divine spellcaster (such as a cleric or paladin), you can use Religion to learn a new spell from the divine tradition. This works like the general Learn a Spell action but uses Religion instead of Arcana, Occultism, or Nature.

To learn the spell:
- Spend 1 hour per level of the spell
- Have materials with the Price indicated in Table 4–3 (Learning a Spell)
- Attempt a Religion check against the DC determined by the GM
