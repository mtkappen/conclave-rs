# Chapter 12: Settings

This chapter covers different time periods, locations, and subgenres available for Call of Cthulhu campaigns.

## Time and Place: The Big Picture

Call of Cthulhu can be set in virtually any era or location where humans exist. The Mythos transcends time and space.

### Choosing a Setting

Consider these factors when selecting a campaign setting:

**Player Interest**: What eras fascinate your group?  
**Available Resources**: Do you have reference materials for the period?  
**Horror Potential**: Does the setting support your desired tone?  
**Technology Level**: Affects available equipment and investigation methods  
**Historical Events**: Real events can provide backdrop or plot hooks  

## Time Periods

### Victorian Era (1837-1901)

**Characteristics:**
- Gaslight, fog, cobblestone streets
- Limited technology but growing industrialization
- Rigid social classes and strict etiquette
- Colonial expansion and exploration

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Gothic atmosphere perfect for horror
- Scientific advancement vs. ancient beliefs
- Hidden underbelly of poverty and crime
- Exploration leading to discovery of ancient things

**Equipment Limitations:**
- Revolvers and early automatic pistols
- No automobiles (horse-drawn carriages)
- Telegraph communication only
- Limited medical knowledge

### Edwardian Era (1901-1914)

**Characteristics:**
- Last days before World War I
- Optimism about progress and technology
- Social change brewing beneath surface
- Golden age of exploration

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Contrast between optimism and horror
- Archaeological discoveries revealing ancient evils
- Early aviation leading to strange encounters
- Pre-war tension adding to atmosphere

### The Roaring Twenties (1920-1929)

**Characteristics:**
- Prohibition and organized crime
- Jazz age, flappers, social liberation
- Economic prosperity before the crash
- Post-WWI disillusionment

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Classic Call of Cthulhu setting
- Speakeasies as cult meeting places
- Crime organizations hiding Mythos connections
- Radio and film bringing new ways to spread horror

**Equipment:**
- Thompson submachine guns, automatic pistols
- Early automobiles (Ford Model T)
- Radios becoming common
- Film cameras for documentation

### The Great Depression (1930-1940)

**Characteristics:**
- Economic hardship and unemployment
- Dust Bowl and environmental disaster
- Rise of fascism abroad
- Desperation making people vulnerable to cults

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Poverty-driven desperation
- Shantytowns hiding strange activities
- Government cover-ups of supernatural events
- People willing to try anything for hope

### World War II Era (1941-1945)

**Characteristics:**
- Global conflict consuming everything
- Advanced technology development
- Espionage and secret operations
- Mass mobilization of society

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Nazi occult research programs
- Military experiments with Mythos artifacts
- War providing cover for cult activities
- Soldiers returning from war changed by what they saw

### Cold War Era (1947-1989)

**Characteristics:**
- Nuclear anxiety and paranoia
- Space race and exploration
- Government conspiracy theories
- Counterculture movements

**Horror Opportunities:**
- CIA/MKUltra-style mind control programs
- Soviet occult research
- UFO sightings and alien contact
- Counterculture exploring forbidden knowledge

### Modern Era (1990-Present)

**Characteristics:**
- Internet and instant communication
- Global connectivity
- Advanced technology everywhere
- Information overload

**Horror Opportunities:**
- Online cults spreading Mythos knowledge
- Deep web hiding dark secrets
- Technology failing in supernatural ways
- Global scale of Mythos threats

**Equipment:**
- Modern firearms and armor
- Cell phones and internet research
- GPS and modern vehicles
- Advanced medical technology

## Subgenres

### Gothic Horror

**Characteristics:**
- Atmospheric, brooding settings
- Ancient castles, mansions, ruins
- Family curses and inherited madness
- Supernatural entities

**Campaign Style:**
- Emphasis on mood and atmosphere
- Personal horror affecting families
- Slow-building dread
- Romantic elements mixed with terror

### Pulp Adventure

**Characteristics:**
- Faster pace, more action
- Heroes who can fight back effectively
- Exotic locations and treasures
- Clear good vs. evil dynamics

**Campaign Style:**
- More combat encounters
- Investigators are capable fighters
- Victory is possible against Mythos threats
- Less emphasis on Sanity loss

### Noir Detective

**Characteristics:**
- Hard-boiled detective fiction style
- Urban settings, crime and corruption
- Moral ambiguity
- Personal stakes for investigators

**Campaign Style:**
- Investigation-focused gameplay
- Criminal underworld connections
- Personal demons of investigators
- Bitter victories at best

### Cosmic Horror

**Characteristics:**
- Emphasis on humanity's insignificance
- Unknowable entities beyond comprehension
- Inevitability of doom
- Knowledge as curse

**Campaign Style:**
- Minimal combat (fighting is futile)
- Maximum Sanity loss and horror
- Escape rather than victory
- Philosophical themes

### Historical Mystery

**Characteristics:**
- Real historical events as backdrop
- Famous figures appear as NPCs
- Investigation of historical mysteries
- Educational elements

**Campaign Style:**
- Research-heavy gameplay
- Period accuracy important
- Historical figures as allies or enemies
- Blending fact and fiction

## Plot Hooks

### Personal Connections

- Missing family member with strange last words
- Inheritance from unknown relative with disturbing conditions
- Childhood friend changed by mysterious experience
- Family curse affecting multiple generations

### Professional Duties

- Academic research leads to dangerous discovery
- Journalist investigating suspicious deaths
- Law enforcement officer noticing pattern of impossible crimes
- Doctor treating patients with strange symptoms

### Accidental Discovery

- Finding strange artifact while moving/renovating
- Witnessing something that shouldn't exist
- Receiving mysterious package or letter
- Stumbling upon secret meeting by accident

### Being Recruited

- Professor at Miskatonic University seeking help
- Government agency recruiting for special division
- Wealthy patron funding investigation
- Religious organization fighting Mythos threats

## Sample Settings

### Arkham, Massachusetts

**Time Period**: 1920s  
**Population**: ~35,000  
**Features:**
- Miskatonic University (major research institution)
- Historic architecture, some dating to colonial era
- Dark corners and abandoned buildings
- Close-knit community with dark secrets

### Innsmouth, Massachusetts

**Time Period**: 1920s  
**Population**: ~500 (declining)  
**Features:**
- Fishing town in decline
- Deep One hybrid population
- Abandoned buildings and empty streets
- Obed Marsh cult legacy

### New York City

**Time Period**: Any  
**Population**: Millions  
**Features:**
- Endless hiding places for cults
- Diverse populations with different traditions
- Financial center (funding for expeditions)
- Museums and institutions with artifacts

### London, England

**Time Period**: Victorian to Modern  
**Population**: Millions  
**Features:**
- British Museum with ancient artifacts
- Colonial connections bringing strange things home
- Underground tunnels and hidden places
- Aristocratic families with dark histories

### Remote Locations

**Examples:**
- Antarctic research stations
- Pacific islands with ancient ruins
- Himalayan monasteries
- Amazon jungle expeditions

**Features:**
- Isolation increases horror
- Limited escape options
- Unknown dangers everywhere
- Ancient civilizations left behind
