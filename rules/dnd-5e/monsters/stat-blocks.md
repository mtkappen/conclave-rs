# Stat Block Overview

Monster stat blocks contain all the information needed to use a creature in combat and other encounters. This section explains how to read and interpret stat blocks.

## Basic Structure

A typical monster stat block includes:

### Name and Size/Type
- **Name:** The creature's name
- **Size:** Tiny, Small, Medium, Large, Huge, or Gargantuan
- **Type:** The creature's type (Aberration, Beast, Celestial, Construct, Dragon, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Giant, Humanoid, Monstrosity, Ooze, Plant, Undead)
- **Alignment:** The creature's typical alignment

### Armor Class (AC)
The number that attacks must meet or exceed to hit the creature. Higher is better.

### Hit Points (HP)
The creature's current and maximum Hit Points. Often shown as a die expression (e.g., 16 (3d8 + 3)).

### Speed
How fast the creature can move, typically in feet per round. May include special speeds like Fly Speed or Swim Speed.

### Ability Scores
The six ability scores with their modifiers:
- **STR** (Strength)
- **DEX** (Dexterity)
- **CON** (Constitution)
- **INT** (Intelligence)
- **WIS** (Wisdom)
- **CHA** (Charisma)

### Saving Throws
Proficient saving throws are listed with their total modifier.

### Skills
Proficient skills are listed with their total modifier.

### Damage Resistances/Vulnerabilities/Immunities
Special rules for how the creature takes damage:
- **Resistance:** Takes half damage
- **Vulnerability:** Takes double damage
- **Immunity:** Takes no damage

### Condition Immunities
Conditions the creature is immune to (Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Incapacitated, Invisible, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Stunned)

### Senses
Special senses and their ranges:
- **Blindsight:** Can perceive without sight
- **Darkvision:** Can see in Darkness as if it were Dim Light
- **Tremorsense:** Can detect vibrations through the ground
- **Truesight:** Can see in Darkness, invisible creatures, and illusions

### Languages
Languages the creature can speak, understand, or read. "—" means no languages. "Telepathy 120 ft." means telepathic communication within 120 feet.

### Challenge Rating (CR)
A measure of how dangerous the creature is, used for determining encounter difficulty and XP rewards.

## Actions

Actions describe what a creature can do on its turn:

### Melee Weapon Attacks
- **Name:** The attack's name
- **Bonus:** The number to add to attack rolls
- **Reach:** How far the attack reaches (typically 5 ft.)
- **Target:** What can be targeted (one creature, etc.)
- **Hit:** Damage dealt on a hit

### Ranged Weapon Attacks
Similar to melee attacks but with range instead of reach.

### Spellcasting
Some creatures can cast spells. Their spell list and spell save DC are included.

## Special Abilities

Many creatures have special abilities that don't fit into standard actions:
- **Legendary Actions:** Powerful actions a legendary creature can take outside its turn
- **Lair Actions:** Actions a creature can take in its lair on initiative count 20
- **Innate Spellcasting:** Natural ability to cast spells without components

## Traits

Traits are passive abilities that affect how the creature functions:
- **Amorphous:** Can move through spaces as small as 1 inch
- **Charge:** Bonus damage if moving a certain distance before attacking
- **Flyby:** Doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks when flying away
- **Hold Breath:** Can hold breath for extended periods
- **Innate Spellcasting:** Natural ability to cast spells
- **Magic Resistance:** Advantage on saving throws against spells
- **Pack Tactics:** Advantage on attack rolls if an ally is near the target
- **Regeneration:** Regains Hit Points at the start of its turn
- **Sunlight Sensitivity/Hostility:** Disadvantage or damage in sunlight

## Understanding Challenge Rating

Challenge Rating indicates how difficult a creature is to defeat:
- **CR 0:** Trivial threat (rats, bats)
- **CR 1/8–1/4:** Minor threats (goblins, kobolds)
- **CR 1–5:** Moderate threats (orcs, ogres)
- **CR 6–10:** Significant threats (trolls, young dragons)
- **CR 11–20:** Major threats (ancient dragons, liches)
- **CR 21+:** Epic threats (Tarrasque, gods)
