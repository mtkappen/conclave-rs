# Medicine (Wisdom)

Medicine measures your ability to provide medical care, treat wounds and diseases, and perform forensic examinations.

## Untrained Actions

### Administer First Aid [two-actions] - HEALING MANIPULATE
You attempt to stabilize a dying creature or stop bleeding. This requires medical supplies or improvised materials like cloth strips.

- **Critical Success:** The creature stabilizes and regains 1d8 Hit Points; if it was dying, its dying value decreases by 2.
- **Success:** The creature stabilizes; if it was dying, its dying value decreases by 1.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the creature's condition doesn't improve.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt harms the creature; it takes 1d4 damage or its dying value increases by 1.

A creature can benefit from only one Administer First Aid attempt per hour, regardless of who attempts it.

### Recall Knowledge
Recall Knowledge about:
- Diseases, poisons, wounds, and forensics
- Medical conditions and treatments
- Anatomy and physiology

## Trained Actions

### Treat Disease - DOWNTIME
You attempt to help a creature recover from a disease. This requires medical supplies and typically takes 1 day per treatment. You can treat multiple patients simultaneously if you have enough supplies and time, but each additional patient imposes a –2 penalty on your check. Make a Medicine check against the disease's DC.

- **Critical Success:** The affected creature receives a +4 circumstance bonus to its next saving throw against the disease.
- **Success:** The affected creature receives a +2 circumstance bonus to its next saving throw against the disease.
- **Failure:** Your treatment has no effect.
- **Critical Failure:** The creature's condition worsens; it takes a –2 penalty to its next saving throw against the disease.

### Treat Poison [two-actions] - HEALING MANIPULATE
You attempt to help a creature recover from poison. This requires antitoxin or other medical supplies. Make a Medicine check against the poison's DC.

- **Critical Success:** The affected creature receives a +4 circumstance bonus to its next saving throw against the poison and regains 1d8 Hit Points.
- **Success:** The affected creature receives a +2 circumstance bonus to its next saving throw against the poison.
- **Failure:** Your treatment has no effect.
- **Critical Failure:** The creature's condition worsens; it takes a –2 penalty to its next saving throw against the poison.

### Treat Wounds - EXPLORATION
You attempt to heal injured creatures using medical supplies. This takes 10 minutes per patient and requires a medical kit or similar supplies. You can treat multiple patients, but each additional patient after the first imposes a –2 penalty on your check. Make a Medicine check against DC 15 (or higher for more severe wounds).

- **Critical Success:** The target regains 3d8 + your level Hit Points; if you're an expert, this increases to 4d8 + your level; master: 5d8 + your level; legendary: 6d8 + your level.
- **Success:** The target regains 1d8 + your level Hit Points; expert: 2d8 + your level; master: 3d8 + your level; legendary: 4d8 + your level.
- **Failure:** Your treatment has no effect.
- **Critical Failure:** The target takes 1d4 damage from your botched treatment.

A creature can benefit from only one Treat Wounds attempt per day, regardless of who attempts it or how many times you try.

**Treat Wounds Healing Table:**
| Proficiency | Critical Success | Success |
|-------------|------------------|---------|
| Trained | 3d8 + level | 1d8 + level |
| Expert | 4d8 + level | 2d8 + level |
| Master | 5d8 + level | 3d8 + level |
| Legendary | 6d8 + level | 4d8 + level |
