# Chapter 11: Stories

This chapter covers adventure design, campaign structure, and provides example scenarios for Call of Cthulhu d20.

## Adventures

An adventure is a self-contained story with a beginning, middle, and end. Good adventures balance investigation, roleplay, and action.

### Adventure Structure

#### The Hook
Draw investigators into the mystery:
- A letter or summons from someone in trouble
- Discovery of strange events or artifacts
- Being hired for a job that seems normal at first
- Personal connection to the mystery (missing relative, etc.)

#### Investigation Phase
Players gather clues and follow leads:
- Multiple paths to discover key information
- Red herrings that lead nowhere
- NPCs who can provide information
- Locations to explore and investigate

#### Rising Action
Danger increases as truth emerges:
- Witnesses found dead or driven mad
- Investigators targeted by cultists
- Discovery of the Mythos connection
- Realization that conventional solutions won't work

#### Climax
Confrontation with the horror:
- Escape rather than victory is often appropriate
- Rituals to be stopped or completed
- Final confrontation with cult leaders or minions
- The Mythos entity itself (usually unwinnable)

#### Resolution
Aftermath of the adventure:
- Survivors deal with trauma and Sanity loss
- Loose ends tied up (or left dangling)
- Rewards for surviving investigators
- Setup for future adventures

### Designing Adventures

#### Clue Design

**Three-Clue Rule**: For every conclusion you want players to reach, provide at least three clues.

**Clue Types:**
- **Physical Evidence**: Documents, objects, bodies
- **Witness Testimony**: NPCs with information
- **Environmental Clues**: Strange phenomena, locations
- **Expert Knowledge**: Skills and knowledge checks

#### Pacing

Balance different types of scenes:
- Quiet investigation (roleplay, research)
- Tense exploration (dangerous locations)
- Action sequences (combat, chases)
- Dramatic revelations (plot twists)

## The First Story

For new groups, start with a simpler adventure:

### Beginner Adventure Guidelines

**Recommended Level**: 1st-3rd level investigators  
**Duration**: 2-4 sessions  
**Complexity**: Single location or small area  

#### Sample Hook Ideas
- A friend has gone missing under strange circumstances
- An inheritance comes with disturbing conditions
- Strange noises from a nearby abandoned building
- A newspaper article about local disappearances

#### First Adventure Goals
- Introduce core mechanics (skills, Sanity)
- Establish the tone of cosmic horror
- Show that not everything can be fought
- Let players succeed at least partially

## The Journey into Mystery

As investigators gain experience, adventures can become more complex:

### Mid-Campaign Adventures

**Recommended Level**: 4th-8th level  
**Duration**: 5-10 sessions  
**Complexity**: Multiple locations, interconnected clues  

#### Themes to Explore
- Personal connections to the Mythos
- Consequences of previous adventures
- Growing knowledge and growing danger
- Moral dilemmas about using forbidden knowledge

### Advanced Adventures

**Recommended Level**: 9th+ level  
**Duration**: 10+ sessions or ongoing campaign  
**Complexity**: Global scope, multiple threads  

#### Themes to Explore
- Investigators as experts in the Mythos
- Race against time to prevent catastrophe
- Personal corruption from Mythos contact
- The cost of knowledge vs. safety of ignorance

## Campaigns

A campaign is a series of connected adventures featuring the same investigators over time.

### Campaign Types

#### Mystery Campaign
Focus on investigation and discovery:
- Each adventure reveals more about a larger mystery
- Investigators build reputation as experts
- Mythos threats escalate gradually

#### Survival Campaign
Emphasis on horror and staying alive:
- Investigators are outmatched by enemies
- Victory means surviving, not defeating
- Sanity management is crucial

#### Power Campaign
Investigators gain knowledge and abilities:
- Learning Mythos magic and rituals
- Building resources and allies
- Eventually confronting major threats

### Campaign Length

**Short Campaign**: 5-10 sessions (one story arc)  
**Medium Campaign**: 10-20 sessions (multiple arcs)  
**Long Campaign**: 20+ sessions (ongoing saga)  

### Character Turnover

In Call of Cthulhu, characters die or go mad frequently:

#### Managing New Characters
- Allow new investigators to join existing groups
- Connect new characters to the story (relatives of fallen allies, etc.)
- Consider legacy mechanics (family lines, organizations)
- Don't let character death stop the campaign

## Red Herrings

Red herrings are false clues that lead nowhere. Use them carefully:

### When to Use Red Herrings

- To add complexity and realism
- To misdirect from obvious conclusions
- To give players false hope before revelation
- To make the real solution more satisfying

### When to Avoid Red Herrings

- Early in a campaign (frustrates new players)
- If they waste too much session time
- If they contradict established facts
- If they make the GM look unprepared

## Non Player Characters

NPCs bring the world to life:

### NPC Types

#### Allies
Helpful characters who aid investigators:
- Contacts with specialized knowledge
- Physical protectors and fighters
- Resources (money, equipment, locations)
- Emotional support and roleplay partners

#### Antagonists
Characters working against investigators:
- Cultists serving Mythos entities
- Rival investigators or organizations
- Corrupt officials or authorities
- Mythos entities themselves

#### Neutral Parties
NPCs with their own agendas:
- Witnesses who may help or hinder
- Authorities who want to contain the situation
- Other groups interested in the same artifacts/knowledge
- Innocents caught in the middle

### Creating Memorable NPCs

**Distinctive Traits:**
- Unique voice or speech pattern
- Physical characteristic (scar, limp, etc.)
- Quirk or habit (nervous tic, always checking watch)
- Strong motivation or goal

**NPC Relationships:**
- Connect NPCs to each other
- Give them history with investigators
- Create factions and rivalries
- Allow relationships to change over time

## Campaign Player Characters

### Investigator Organizations

Groups that investigators can belong to:

#### Miskatonic University
Academic institution in Arkham:
- Access to libraries and experts
- Funding for research expeditions
- Prestige and connections
- Political complications

#### Government Agencies
Official organizations with resources:
- FBI, Secret Service (modern)
- Bureau of Investigation (1920s)
- Military intelligence
- Special divisions dealing with supernatural

#### Private Organizations
Independent groups:
- Occult societies
- Wealthy patron's retinue
- Investigator networks
- Family organizations

### Campaign Player Characters

Some players may want to run multiple characters over a campaign:

**Advantages:**
- Continuity when characters die
- Deeper connection to campaign world
- Ability to specialize different characters

**Disadvantages:**
- More bookkeeping for player
- Potential for power gaming
- Less focus on individual characters

## Sample Adventure Hooks

### The End of Paradise (Adventure 1)

**Premise**: A wealthy recluse invites investigators to his island estate, but something is terribly wrong.

**Hook**: Invitation promising fortune and answers about missing family member  
**Setting**: Isolated island mansion  
**Threat**: Deep One hybrid cult  
**Climax**: Escape from the island before ritual completion  

### Little Slices of Death (Adventure 2)

**Premise**: A series of gruesome murders in a small town point to something ancient awakening beneath.

**Hook**: Newspaper reports of impossible murder methods  
**Setting**: Rural New England town  
**Threat**: Cult of Tsathoggua  
**Climax**: Stop the ritual before the god's avatar manifests
