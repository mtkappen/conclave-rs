# Chapter 7: Magic

Magic in Call of Cthulhu d20 represents the dangerous knowledge of the Cthulhu Mythos. Unlike traditional fantasy magic, Mythos magic is alien, corrupting, and comes at a terrible cost to the caster's Sanity and mental abilities.

## Artifacts and Tomes

Artifacts are physical objects imbued with Mythos power. Tomes are books containing forbidden knowledge.

### Handling Artifacts

When characters encounter artifacts or read Mythos tomes, strange events may occur:

| 1d20 Roll | Event                                                                 |
|-----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1         | Character hears whispering in unknown language                        |
| 2         | Shadows move independently of light sources                           |
| 3         | Character experiences vivid nightmares for 1d4 nights                 |
| 4         | Objects in vicinity move slightly when unobserved                     |
| 5         | Character feels watched by unseen presence                            |
| 6         | Temperature drops dramatically in localized area                      |
| 7         | Electronic devices malfunction or behave erratically                  |
| 8         | Animals become agitated near the artifact                             |
| 9         | Character sees fleeting glimpses of non-Euclidean geometry            |

| 11        | Character experiences déjà vu repeatedly                              |

| 18        | Character feels overwhelming sense of dread                           |
| 19        | Reality seems to "glitch" momentarily                                 |

### Books of the Cthulhu Mythos

#### The Necronomicon

**Author**: Abdul Alhazred (the "Mad Arab")  
**Original Language**: Arabic (730 AD)  
**Translations**: Latin (1228), Greek (1350s), English, German, French  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 4d6 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d6/1d10 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d8+2

**Contents**: Comprehensive knowledge of the Great Old Ones, their histories, and basic summoning rituals. The most famous and dangerous of all Mythos tomes.

#### The Book of Eibon

**Author**: Hyperborean sorcerer Eibon  
**Original Language**: Unknown (pre-human)  
**Translations**: Greek, Latin, French  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 3d6 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d4/1d8 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d6+2

**Contents**: Spells and rituals of the Hyperborean civilization, including powerful transformation magic.

#### Unausprechlichen Kulten (Nameless Cults)

**Author**: Friedrich von Junzt  
**Original Language**: German (1839)  
**Translations**: English, French, Italian  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 2d6 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d4/1d6 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d6+1

**Contents**: Detailed information on Mythos cults, their rituals, and locations. Von Junzt was executed by the Prussian secret police after completing this work.

#### De Vermis Mysteriis (Mysteries of the Worm)

**Author**: Ludvig Prihn  
**Original Language**: Latin (1648)  
**Translations**: German, English  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 3d4 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d4/1d8 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d6

**Contents**: Focuses on necromancy and the worship of worm-like entities. Contains rituals for communicating with the dead.

#### The King in Yellow

**Author**: Unknown (possibly Hastur-related)  
**Original Language**: French  
**Translations**: English, German  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 2d6 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d8/1d10 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d4+2

**Contents**: A play that drives readers mad. Contains information about Hastur and the city of Carcosa. Merely reading Act I can cause insanity.

#### The Pnakotic Manuscripts

**Author**: The Pnakotus (ancient race)  
**Original Language**: Unknown  
**Translations**: Fragmentary translations exist  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 6d6 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d6/1d20 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d10

**Contents**: The history of Earth before humanity, including the rise and fall of pre-human civilizations.

#### Cultes des Goules

**Author**: Count d'Erlette  
**Original Language**: French (1752)  
**Translations**: Latin, English  

**Effects:**
- **Examination Period**: 3d4 weeks per reading
- **Sanity Loss**: 1d6/1d8 per reading
- **Cthulhu Mythos Ranks Gained**: 1d6+1

**Contents**: Information on ghouls, their society, and how to communicate with them. Also contains necromantic rituals.

#### Unaussprechlichen Kulten (Alternate Version)

Various versions exist with different contents and dangers. GM should determine specific effects based on campaign needs.

### Non-Book Artifacts

#### Shining Trapezohedron

**Description**: A gemstone that can trap or release entities from the void  
**Effects**: Can summon or banish entities at GM's discretion  
**Sanity Loss**: 1d8 per use  

#### Deep One Breather

**Description**: Device allowing humans to breathe underwater  
**Effects**: Grants water breathing for up to 8 hours  
**Sanity Loss**: 0/1d4 (first use only)  

#### Mi-Go Equipment

**Description**: Various devices created by the Mi-Go fungi from Yuggoth  
**Effects**: Varies widely; includes brain cylinders, anti-gravity devices  
**Sanity Loss**: 1d6 per significant interaction  

## Casting Spells

Mythos spells are dangerous rituals that draw power from cosmic entities. They require careful preparation and exact execution.

### What is a Spell?

A spell is a single magical effect created by manipulating Mythos forces. Each spell has specific components, casting time, range, target, duration, and effects.

### Casting Time

Most spells require significant time to cast:
- **Standard**: 10 minutes minimum
- **Complex Rituals**: 1 hour to several days
- **Quick Spells**: Some can be cast in 1 round (rare)

Spells often require specific times (moon phases, planetary alignments) or locations (sacred spaces, crossroads).

### Range

| Range Type    | Distance                              |
|---------------|---------------------------------------|
| Personal      | Only the caster                       |
| Touch         | Caster must touch target              |
| Close         | 25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels               |
| Medium        | 100 ft. + 10 ft./level                |
| Long          | 400 ft. + 40 ft./level                |

### Target/Effect/Area

Spells affect targets, create effects, or cover areas:
- **Target**: Specific creature(s) or object(s)
- **Effect**: Creates a magical effect in a location
- **Area**: Affects all within a defined area (burst, emanation, etc.)

### Duration

| Duration Type | Description                                                                 |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Instantaneous | Effect occurs and ends immediately                                          |
| Permanent     | Lasts until dispelled or countered                                          |
| Concentration | Lasts as long as caster concentrates (up to 1 round/level)                  |
| Fixed         | Specific duration (minutes, hours, days)                                    |

### Saving Throws

Most spells allow saving throws:
- **Will**: Mental resistance against mind-affecting effects
- **Fortitude**: Physical resistance against harmful effects
- **Reflex**: Dexterity-based resistance against area effects

**Saving Throw DC**: 10 + spell level + caster's relevant ability modifier

### Spell Components

Mythos spells require various components:

#### Verbal (V)

Incantations, chants, or speaking in unknown languages. Some spells require specific words of power.

#### Somatic (S)

Gestures, hand movements, or ritual dances. May require free hands or specific implements.

#### Material (M)

Physical components consumed during casting:
- Rare herbs and chemicals
- Animal parts
- Precious metals and gems
- Blood sacrifices (for powerful spells)

**Material Component Cost**: Varies by spell; typically 10-500 gp worth of materials

#### Focus (F)

Special items required for casting:
- Magical tomes
- Ritual implements
- Specific artifacts

Focus components are not consumed but must be possessed during casting.

### Concentration

Casters must maintain concentration to complete many spells:
- Make Concentration check if damaged while casting
- DC = 10 + damage taken + spell level
- Failure means spell is lost

### Caster Level

Caster level determines the potency of a spell:
- **Mythos Caster Level**: Based on Cthulhu Mythos skill ranks
- **Spell Power**: Many spells scale with caster level
- **Duration/Range**: Often based on caster level

### Multiple Casters

Multiple casters can combine their efforts for more powerful rituals:
- Each additional caster adds +1 to effective caster level
- All must participate in the ritual
- Sanity costs are divided among participants (GM discretion)

### Spell Failure

Spells can fail due to:
- **Incorrect components**: Missing required materials
- **Distractions**: Interrupted concentration
- **Wrong conditions**: Incorrect time, location, or phase
- **Failed checks**: Required skill checks not met

**Consequences of Failure**: Sanity loss, backlash damage, or attracting unwanted attention from Mythos entities.

### Learning Spells

Spells are learned through:
1. **Reading Tomes**: Primary method; costs Sanity and Cthulhu Mythos ranks
2. **Teaching**: Another caster can teach (requires both to have the spell)
3. **Discovery**: Finding spell scrolls or notes (rare)

**Spell Learning Cost**: 
- Sanity loss from reading source material
- Time for study and practice
- Possible skill checks for complex spells

### Combining Magical Effects

Multiple magical effects can overlap:
- **Same Effect**: Best bonus applies; they don't stack
- **Different Effects**: Bonuses typically stack unless same type
- **Conflicting Effects**: GM determines which prevails

## Mythos Spells

Mythos spells are categorized by their purpose and effect. Below are examples of common spell types:

### Divination Spells

**Augury**  
*Divination, Level 1*  
**Casting Time**: 10 minutes  
**Components**: V, S, M (incense worth 25 gp)  
**Duration**: Instantaneous  
**Saving Throw**: None  

You receive an omen about whether a particular course of action will bring good or bad results.

**Detect Magic**  
*Divination, Level 1*  
**Casting Time**: 1 round  
**Components**: V, S  
**Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 min/level  
**Saving Throw**: None  

You detect the presence of magical auras in the area.

**Contact Other Plane**  
*Divination, Level 5*  
**Casting Time**: 1 hour  
**Components**: V, S, M (rare incense)  
**Duration**: Instantaneous  
**Saving Throw**: Will partial  

You contact an extraplanar entity to ask questions. Risk of mental damage if entity is hostile.

### Summoning Spells

**Contact Creature**  
*Conjuration, Level 3*  
**Casting Time**: 10 minutes  
**Components**: V, S, M (blood sacrifice)  
**Duration**: 1 hour/level

**Summon Monster (Mythos)**

## Spell Costs and Consequences

### Ability Score Loss

Many Mythos spells require the caster to sacrifice ability points:
- **Temporary Loss**: Points return after 1d4 days
- **Permanent Loss**: Points are gone forever (unless restored by powerful magic)

### Sanity Costs

Every Mythos spell costs Sanity:
- **Minor Spells**: 0/1d3
- **Moderate Spells**: 1/1d6
- **Major Spells**: 1d4/1d10
- **Epic Rituals**: 1d10/1d100

### GM Advice on Magic

**Use Sparingly**: Mythos magic should be rare and dangerous. Players should hesitate before using it.

**Costly Victory**: Every spell cast brings the character closer to madness or death.

**Unknown Consequences**: The GM should occasionally introduce unexpected side effects from spellcasting.
