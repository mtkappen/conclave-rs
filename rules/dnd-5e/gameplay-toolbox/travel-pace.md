# Travel Pace

When traveling outside combat, a group can move at different paces depending on how quickly they need to travel and how cautious they want to be.

## Travel Paces

| Pace | Distance Per Minute | Distance Per Hour | Distance Per Day |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Fast | 400 feet | 4 miles | 30 miles |
| Normal | 300 feet | 3 miles | 24 miles |
| Slow | 200 feet | 2 miles | 18 miles |

### Fast Pace
- Characters move quickly but are less observant
- Travelers have Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight
- Travelers have Disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks

### Normal Pace
- Balanced speed and awareness
- No special modifiers to checks

### Slow Pace
- Characters move carefully and deliberately
- Travelers have Advantage on Wisdom (Perception or Survival) checks
- Travelers have Disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks due to slower movement

## Mounts and Vehicles

### Mounted Travel
If riding horses or other mounts, the group can move twice the distance shown in the table for 1 hour. After that, mounts need a Short or Long Rest before they can move at that increased pace again.

### Waterborne Travel
Characters in waterborne vessels are limited to the speed of the vessel and don't choose a travel pace. Depending on the vessel and crew size, ships might be able to travel for up to 24 hours per day.

### Land Vehicles
Travelers in wagons, carriages, or other land vehicles choose a pace as normal. The vehicle's speed may limit how fast you can travel.

## Marching Order

The adventurers should establish a marching order while they travel, whether indoors or outdoors. A marching order makes it easier to determine:
- Which characters are affected by traps
- Which ones can spot hidden enemies
- Which ones are closest to those enemies if a fight breaks out

You can change your marching order outside combat and record the order any way you like (write it down, arrange miniatures, etc.).

## Foraging

While traveling, characters can forage for food. The success of foraging depends on:
- **Environment:** Deserts and arctic regions are harder to forage in than forests
- **Wisdom (Survival) Check:** DC varies by environment (typically 12–15)
- **Travel Pace:** Foraging while traveling at a Slow pace may grant Advantage

## Navigation

Characters can navigate using:
- **Maps and Compasses:** Standard navigation tools
- **Wisdom (Survival) Check:** To find the way in wild terrain
- **Intelligence (Nature) Check:** To identify landmarks and celestial bodies

Failing a navigation check might cause the group to become lost, requiring additional time and resources to reach their destination.

## Forced March

Characters can travel beyond the normal day's limit by taking extra hours of travel:
- Each extra hour requires a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 + 1 per extra hour)
- Failure results in one level of Exhaustion
- A Long Rest removes all levels of Exhaustion gained from forced marching
