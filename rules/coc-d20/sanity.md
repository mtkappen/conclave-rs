# Chapter 4: Sanity

Sanity represents a character's mental stability and resistance to the horrors of the Cthulhu Mythos. Unlike other d20 games, Call of Cthulhu features Sanity as a core mechanic that tracks the psychological toll of encountering cosmic horrors.

## Starting Sanity

A character's starting Sanity score equals her Wisdom score. This represents her initial mental resilience before encountering the horrors of the Mythos.

## Loss of Sanity

When a character encounters something truly horrifying, she may lose Sanity points. The amount lost depends on the severity of the encounter.

### Types of Sanity Loss

1. **Temporary Sanity Loss**: Immediate reaction to horror
2. **Indefinite Insanity**: Long-term mental damage from severe trauma

### Common Sanity Loss Values

| Encounter Type                              | Sanity Loss      |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------|
| Seeing a corpse or gruesome murder          | 0/1d3            |
| Witnessing a violent death                  | 0/1d4            |
| Encountering a monstrous creature           | 0/1d6            |
| Reading a Mythos tome (first time)          | 1d6/1d10         |
| Direct contact with a Great Old One         | 1d10/1d100       |
| Witnessing reality-shattering events        | 1d8/1d20         |

The first number is temporary Sanity loss; the second (after the slash) is potential indefinite insanity loss.

## Sanity Points

Sanity points function similarly to hit points but for mental stability:

- **Maximum Sanity**: Equals your Wisdom score
- **Current Sanity**: Starts at maximum, decreases with horror encounters
- **Zero Sanity**: Character becomes hopelessly insane and is no longer playable

### Getting Used to Awfulness

Repeated exposure to similar horrors reduces Sanity loss. Once you've lost Sanity to a particular type of encounter, subsequent encounters with the same type cause only 1 point of temporary Sanity loss (unless the new encounter is significantly worse).

**Example**: After losing Sanity seeing a Deep One, encountering another Deep One later causes only 1 point of temporary loss instead of the full amount.

## Insanity

When a character loses enough Sanity at once, she may suffer from temporary or indefinite insanity.

### Temporary Insanity

If you lose more than half your current Sanity points in a single encounter (round down), you must roll on the Temporary Insanity table.

#### Duration of Temporary Insanity

| 1d6 Roll | Duration        |
|----------|-----------------|
| 1        | 1d10 minutes    |
| 2        | 1d10 hours      |
| 3        | 1d4 days        |
| 4        | 1d6 weeks       |
| 5        | 1d8 months      |
| 6        | 1 year          |

#### Short-Term Temporary Insanity Effects

| 1d20 Roll | Effect                                                                 |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1-3       | Catatonia: Character is unresponsive, staring blankly                  |
| 4-6       | Panic Attack: Character flees in terror for the duration               |
| 7-9       | Hysteria: Character laughs or cries uncontrollably                     |
| 10-12     | Paranoia: Character believes everyone is out to get her                |
| 13-15     | Phobia: Develops a specific phobia related to the horror encountered   |
| 16-18     | Amnesia: Character forgets recent events or personal identity          |
| 19-20     | Delusion: Character believes something completely false                |

#### Long-Term Temporary Insanity Effects

For more severe cases, use these effects instead:

| 1d20 Roll | Effect                                                                 |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1-4       | Multiple Personality: Character develops an alternate personality      |
| 5-8       | Obsession: Character becomes obsessed with a specific object or idea   |
| 9-12      | Depression: Character suffers -4 penalty to all actions                |
| 13-16     | Mania: Character acts recklessly and impulsively                       |
| 17-20     | Psychosomatic Illness: Character develops physical symptoms with no cause |

### Indefinite Insanity

If you lose Sanity equal to or greater than your current Sanity score in a single encounter, you suffer indefinite insanity. This is permanent mental damage that requires professional treatment.

#### Random Indefinite Insanity Effects

| 1d20 Roll | Effect                                                                 |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1         | Schizophrenia: Character hears voices and sees things that aren't there |
| 2         | Bipolar Disorder: Character alternates between depression and mania    |
| 3         | Severe PTSD: Character has flashbacks and panic attacks                |
| 4         | Dissociative Identity Disorder: Multiple distinct personalities        |
| 5         | Paranoid Delusions: Character believes in elaborate conspiracy theories |
| 6         | Catatonic Depression: Character rarely speaks or moves                 |
| 7         | Manic Depression: Extreme mood swings without trigger                  |
| 8         | Severe Phobia: Debilitating fear of common objects or situations       |
| 9         | Compulsive Behavior: Character must perform rituals repeatedly         |
| 10        | Narcissistic Personality: Character has grandiose sense of self-importance |
| 11        | Antisocial Personality: Character lacks empathy and remorse            |
| 12        | Borderline Personality: Character has unstable relationships and self-image |
| 13-15     | Chronic Anxiety: Constant worry and fear affecting daily life          |
| 16-18     | Substance Abuse: Character turns to drugs or alcohol to cope           |
| 19-20     | Suicidal Ideation: Character frequently contemplates self-harm         |

## Insane Insight

Characters with high Wisdom may gain insights from their Sanity loss. When a character recovers from temporary insanity, the GM may award her knowledge about the Mythos or the specific horror she encountered.

### Recovering Sanity

Sanity can be recovered through various means:

1. **Natural Recovery**: 1 point per week of rest and safety
2. **Psychoanalysis**: Successful Heal or Psychoanalysis checks can restore Sanity
3. **Mythos Magic**: Certain spells can restore Sanity (usually at a cost)
4. **Achievement**: Overcoming significant Mythos threats may restore Sanity

#### Recovery Rates

| Method                    | Amount Restored    | Time Required     |
|---------------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Natural recovery          | 1 point            | 1 week            |
| Successful Psychoanalysis | 1d4 points         | 1 month           |
| Hospitalization           | 1d6 points         | 3 months          |
| Mythos magic              | Varies             | Varies            |

## Treatment of Insanity

Professional treatment can help characters recover from insanity, though the process is lengthy and uncertain.

### Mental Disorders

Characters who suffer from indefinite insanity develop mental disorders that persist until treated. These disorders affect gameplay in various ways:

- **Social Interaction**: Penalties on Diplomacy, Bluff, and other social skills
- **Combat**: Penalties on initiative, attack rolls, or saving throws
- **Skill Checks**: Specific skill penalties based on the disorder
- **Roleplaying**: GM may impose behavioral restrictions

### A Psychiatric Glossary

#### Addictions

Characters may develop addictions to substances as a coping mechanism:

| Substance     | Withdrawal DC | Effects of Withdrawal              |
|---------------|---------------|------------------------------------|
| Alcohol       | 10            | -2 on all checks for 1d4 days      |
| Opium         | 15            | -4 on all checks, 1d6 damage/day   |
| Cocaine       | 18            | Severe depression, -6 on all checks|
| Morphine      | 20            | Intense pain, -8 on all checks     |

#### Medications and Treatments

Modern (or period-appropriate) psychiatric treatments include:

- **Talk Therapy**: Regular sessions with a psychiatrist
- **Medication**: Antidepressants, antipsychotics, mood stabilizers
- **Hospitalization**: Inpatient care for severe cases
- **Electroconvulsive Therapy**: For treatment-resistant depression
- **Rest Cure**: Extended period of rest and isolation

### Nightgaunt Syndrome

A specific condition affecting those who have encountered certain Mythos entities. Symptoms include:

- Fear of heights
- Nightmares about flying
- Inability to sleep without fear
- -4 penalty on Will saves against fear effects

## Sanity and the Cthulhu Mythos Skill

The Cthulhu Mythos skill represents a character's growing understanding of the cosmic horrors that exist beyond normal human comprehension. Unlike other skills:

- **Cannot be purchased**: Must be gained through reading Mythos tomes or direct encounters
- **No maximum rank**: Can increase without limit
- **Costs Sanity**: Each rank gained costs 1d6 points of maximum Sanity

### Gaining Cthulhu Mythos Ranks

| Method                          | Ranks Gained    | Sanity Cost     |
|---------------------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Reading a minor Mythos tome     | 1d4             | 1d6             |
| Reading a major Mythos tome     | 1d6+2           | 1d10            |
| Direct encounter with entity    | 1d3             | 1d8             |
| Study under knowledgeable mentor| 1d2             | 1d4             |

## GM Advice: Sanity in Play

### When to Call for Sanity Loss

- **First encounters**: Always call for Sanity loss when characters first encounter Mythos horrors
- **Escalating horror**: Increase Sanity loss as the threat becomes more apparent
- **Personal connection**: Greater Sanity loss if the horror affects someone the character knows
- **Helplessness**: More Sanity loss if characters feel powerless against the threat

### Balancing Sanity Loss

- Don't drain Sanity too quickly, or players will lose characters constantly
- Provide opportunities for Sanity recovery between adventures
- Consider the campaign tone: Horror-focused games should have more Sanity loss
- Reward clever play that avoids unnecessary horror exposure
