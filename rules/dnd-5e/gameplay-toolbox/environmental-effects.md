# Environmental Effects

The world of D&D is filled with diverse environments that can affect characters in various ways. This section covers common environmental challenges and their rules.

## Weather

Weather can impact travel, combat, and survival:

### Extreme Cold
- **Temperature:** Below 0°F (or below freezing in moderate climates)
- **Effect:** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw at the end of each hour or gain one level of Exhaustion
- **Protection:** Warm clothing, fire, shelter, or magical warmth can prevent this

### Extreme Heat
- **Temperature:** Above 100°F (or above normal in hot climates)
- **Effect:** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw at the end of each hour or gain one level of Exhaustion
- **Protection:** Adequate water, shade, or magical cooling can prevent this

### Wind
- **Moderate Wind:** Disadvantage on ranged weapon attack rolls and Perception checks that rely on hearing
- **Strong Wind:** Ranged weapon attacks are impossible; loud speech is difficult
- **Severe Wind:** Can knock creatures prone; flying creatures must succeed on Strength checks or be blown off course

### Rain and Snow
- **Light:** No significant effect
- **Moderate:** Disadvantage on Perception checks that rely on sight or hearing
- **Heavy:** Heavily obscures areas; fire-based effects may be extinguished

## Terrain Types

### Difficult Terrain
Movement through difficult terrain costs extra movement:
- **Examples:** Sand, shallow bogs, thick undergrowth, stairs, rubble
- **Effect:** Every foot of movement costs 1 extra foot
- **Flying Creatures:** Often ignore nonmagical difficult terrain

### Dangerous Terrain
Some terrain can harm characters:
- **Thorns/Spikes:** May deal piercing damage when moved through
- **Ice/Slick Surfaces:** May require Dexterity checks to avoid falling prone
- **Quicksand/Mud:** Can restrain or slow movement

## Visibility and Light

### Darkness
- **Heavily Obscured:** Creatures have the Blinded condition when trying to see something in darkness
- **Darkvision:** Allows seeing in darkness as if it were dim light (within specified range)
- **Magical Darkness:** Blocks darkvision unless specifically stated otherwise

### Dim Light
- **Lightly Obscured:** Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight
- Created by twilight, dawn, moonlight, or weak artificial light

### Fog and Mist
- **Light Fog:** Lightly obscures a 10–30 foot radius
- **Heavy Fog/Cloud:** Heavily obscures areas, creating total concealment

## Water

### Swimming
- **Swim Speed:** Allows movement through water at full speed
- **No Swim Speed:** Movement costs extra (typically 2 feet for every 1 foot moved)
- **Breathing:** Characters without a swim speed or water breathing need to surface regularly

### Underwater Combat
- **Melee Attacks:** Disadvantage unless the weapon deals Piercing damage and the attacker has a Swim Speed
- **Ranged Attacks:** Automatically miss beyond normal range; disadvantage within normal range
- **Fire Damage:** Anything underwater has Resistance to Fire damage

## Altitude

### High Altitude
- **Above 5,000 feet:** Characters may need to acclimate or suffer from altitude sickness
- **Effects:** Disadvantage on physical ability checks and attack rolls; reduced movement
- **Breathing:** Some creatures can't function at extreme altitudes

## Natural Hazards

### Avalanches
- **Warning Signs:** Wisdom (Survival) check to detect danger
- **Effect:** Creatures caught in avalanches take bludgeoning damage and may be buried

### Earthquakes
- **Duration:** Typically 1d4 rounds
- **Effects:** Structures may collapse; creatures must make Dexterity saves or fall prone
- **Aftermath:** Aftershocks, landslides, tsunamis (near water)

### Lightning Storms
- **Warning:** Wisdom (Nature) check to predict incoming storms
- **Effect:** Lightning can strike tall objects and open areas
- **Protection:** Seek shelter in buildings or caves; avoid metal objects

## Survival Checks

Wisdom (Survival) checks are commonly used for:
- Tracking creatures through terrain
- Foraging for food and water
- Predicting weather changes
- Navigating without maps
- Avoiding natural hazards
- Building temporary shelters

### DC Guidelines
- **Very Easy:** 5 (clear tracks, obvious shelter)
- **Easy:** 10 (fresh tracks, basic shelter)
- **Medium:** 15 (old tracks, good shelter in difficult terrain)
- **Hard:** 20 (stale tracks, excellent shelter in extreme conditions)
- **Very Hard:** 25 (nearly invisible tracks, perfect shelter in dangerous area)

## Exhaustion

Environmental hazards often cause Exhaustion, which has six levels:
1. Disadvantage on ability checks
2. Speed halved
3. Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws
4. Hit Point maximum halved
5. Speed reduced to 0
6. Death

Most effects that cause exhaustion specify how many levels are gained. A Long Rest removes one level of exhaustion (unless caused by certain extreme conditions).
