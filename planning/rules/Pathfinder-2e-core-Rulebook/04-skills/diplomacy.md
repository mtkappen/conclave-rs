# Diplomacy (Charisma)

Diplomacy measures your ability to interact with others, make good impressions, and negotiate.

## Untrained Actions

### Gather Information - EXPLORATION
You spend time talking with people in a settlement or area to learn about a topic, person, or event. This typically takes 1d4 hours per check, and the GM sets the DC based on how well-known the information is and how accessible it is to the general public.

- **Critical Success:** You gather comprehensive and accurate information quickly; you might also learn related details you weren't specifically looking for.
- **Success:** You gather useful and generally accurate information about your topic.
- **Failure:** You gather little or no useful information, or what you learn is incomplete or slightly inaccurate.
- **Critical Failure:** You gather misleading or false information, or you attract unwanted attention with your inquiries.

### Make an Impression - CONCENTRATE
**Requirements:** You can see and communicate with the target.

You attempt to make a good impression on a creature to improve its attitude toward you. This takes at least 1 minute of interaction (such as conversation, performance, or gift-giving). Make a Diplomacy check against the target's Will DC. The GM may adjust the DC based on the target's current disposition and any relevant circumstances.

- **Critical Success:** The target's attitude improves by two steps (from unfriendly to indifferent, for example).
- **Success:** The target's attitude improves by one step.
- **Failure:** The target's attitude remains unchanged.
- **Critical Failure:** The target's attitude worsens by one step.

**Attitude Levels:**
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Hostile | Will take risks to harm you |
| Unfriendly | Wishes you ill |
| Indifferent | Neither helps nor harms you |
| Friendly | Wishes you well |
| Helpful | Takes risks to help you |

### Request [one-action]
**Requirements:** You can see and communicate with the target.

You attempt to convince a creature to do something for you. This works similarly to Make an Impression, but focuses on a specific action rather than general attitude. The GM sets the DC based on how reasonable the request is and the target's current attitude toward you.

- **Critical Success:** The target agrees to your request enthusiastically.
- **Success:** The target agrees to your request.
- **Failure:** The target refuses your request.
- **Critical Failure:** The target not only refuses but becomes offended or hostile.

**Request DC Examples:**
| Request Type | Typical DC (Friendly Target) |
|--------------|------------------------------|
| Minor favor | 10-15 |
| Moderate request | 15-20 |
| Significant favor | 20-25 |
| Dangerous or illegal act | 25+ |
