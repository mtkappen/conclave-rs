# Monk

You are a master of martial arts and inner energy, using speed, precision, and ki to strike with supernatural efficiency. Monks rely on Dexterity and Wisdom, excelling at mobility and unarmed combat.

## Core Monk Traits

- **Primary Ability:** Dexterity and Wisdom
- **Hit Point Die:** D8 per Monk level
- **Saving Throws:** Strength and Dexterity

### Proficiencies
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Choose 2: Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, or Stealth
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons and Martial weapons with the Light property
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Choose one type of Artisan's Tools or Musical Instrument
- **Armor Training:** None

### Starting Equipment
Choose A or B:
- (A) Spear, 5 Daggers, Artisan's Tools or Musical Instrument, Explorer's Pack, and 11 GP
- (B) 50 GP

## Class Features

### Level 1: Martial Arts
You are trained in unarmed combat. You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for attack and damage rolls with Unarmed Strikes and Monk weapons (Simple weapons and Martial weapons with the Light property). When you hit with an Unarmed Strike or Monk weapon attack on your turn, you can roll 1d6 + your Dexterity modifier as bonus damage. The die increases to d8 at level 5, d10 at level 11, and d12 at level 17.

You can draw or stow one Monk weapon when you make an unarmed attack as part of the same action.

### Level 1: Unarmored Defense
While you aren't wearing any armor or wielding a Shield, your base Armor Class equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier.

### Level 2: Monk's Focus
You can channel your inner energy (ki) to fuel special abilities. You gain a number of Focus Points equal to your Monk level. Many features require you to spend Focus Points to activate.

### Level 2: Unarmored Movement
Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren't wearing armor or wielding a Shield. This bonus increases as you level up (+15 at level 6, +20 at level 9, etc.).

### Level 2: Uncanny Metabolism
You have Advantage on Death Saving Throws. When you roll 18–20 on a Death Saving Throw, you gain the benefit of rolling a 20. At the start of each turn when you're Bloodied and have at least 1 Hit Point, regain Hit Points equal to 5 + Constitution modifier.

### Level 3: Deflect Attacks
When you are hit by an attack or take damage from a ranged attack, you can use your Reaction to reduce the damage by 1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Monk level. If you reduce the damage to 0, you can catch the projectile and make a ranged attack with it as a Bonus Action.

### Level 3: Monk Subclass
Choose a subclass (Monastic Tradition).

### Level 4: Ability Score Improvement
You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice. You gain this feature again at levels 8, 12, 16, and 19.

### Level 4: Slow Fall
When you fall, you can use your Reaction to reduce falling damage by an amount equal to 5 + your Monk level.

### Level 5: Extra Attack
You can attack twice instead of once whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

### Level 5: Stunning Strike
When you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike or Monk weapon attack, you can spend 1 Focus Point to force it to make a Constitution saving throw. On a failure, the target is Stunned until the end of your next turn.

### Level 6: Empowered Strikes
Your Unarmed Strikes deal Force damage instead of their normal damage type. The damage die increases to d8.

### Level 7: Evasion
When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed and half damage if you fail.

### Level 9: Acrobatic Movement
You can move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the movement.

### Level 14: Diamond Soul
You have proficiency in all saving throws. Additionally, whenever you make a saving throw and fail, you can spend 2 Focus Points to reroll it and must use the new roll.

### Level 18: Timeless Body
The primal magic that you wield causes you to age more slowly. For every ten years that pass, your body ages only one year. You also don't need to eat or breathe while in your form.

### Level 20: Empty Body
You can spend 4 Focus Points as a Bonus Action to gain the following benefits for 1 minute:
- You have Resistance to all damage types except Force and Psychic
- You can move through other creatures and objects as if they were Difficult Terrain (take 1d10 Force damage if you end your turn inside an object)
