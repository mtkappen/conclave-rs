# Crafting (Intelligence)

Crafting measures your ability to create items, from simple tools to complex magical devices.

## Untrained Actions

### Recall Knowledge
Recall Knowledge about:
- Alchemical reactions and creatures
- Item value
- Engineering
- Unusual materials
- Constructs

## Trained Actions

### Craft - DOWNTIME
You create an item using raw materials. This is a complex downtime activity that requires appropriate tools, materials worth at least half the item's price, and time based on the item's level and complexity. The GM sets the DC based on the item's level and rarity.

**Process:**
1. **Purchase Materials:** Buy raw materials worth at least half the item's final price.
2. **First Check:** After 1 day of work, attempt a Crafting check against the item's DC.
3. **Progress:** Each day of work advances the item's progress based on your result.
4. **Completion:** When you've accumulated enough success days, pay the remaining cost and complete the item.

- **Critical Success:** You complete the item more quickly or with higher quality than normal; you might reduce the material cost by 10%.
- **Success:** You make progress toward completing the item according to the standard timeline.
- **Failure:** You make no progress that day, and might waste some materials.
- **Critical Failure:** You ruin the materials and must start over with new ones.

**Item DC Examples:**
| Item Level | Typical DC |
|------------|------------|
| 0-2 (Common items) | 15-18 |
| 3-6 (Uncommon items) | 18-23 |
| 7-10 (Rare items) | 23-28 |
| 11+ (Legendary items) | 28+ |

### Earn Income - DOWNTIME
You use Crafting to produce goods for sale. This works like the general Earn Income activity, but specifically creates items you can sell at full price rather than just receiving wages.

- **Critical Success:** You create high-quality work that fetches a premium price; earn income as if the task were 1 level higher.
- **Success:** You produce quality goods that sell for standard prices.
- **Failure:** Your work is shoddy and sells at a discount or might not sell at all.
- **Critical Failure:** Your products are defective and worthless; you lose some materials in the process.

### Identify Alchemy - EXPLORATION
You attempt to identify an alchemical item's properties without using it. This typically requires examining the item closely and might take 10 minutes or more. Make a Crafting check against a DC set by the GM based on the item's level and rarity.

- **Critical Success:** You correctly identify the item and all its properties, including any special uses or activation methods.
- **Success:** You correctly identify the item and its primary function.
- **Failure:** You can't determine what the item does.
- **Critical Failure:** You misidentify the item, potentially with dangerous consequences if you use it incorrectly.

### Repair - EXPLORATION
You attempt to repair a damaged object. This requires appropriate tools and materials. Make a Crafting check against the DC set by the GM based on the object's level and damage extent.

- **Critical Success:** You restore the object to full functionality and might even improve it slightly.
- **Success:** You restore some or all of the object's functionality, removing the broken condition if applicable.
- **Failure:** Your repair attempt fails; the object remains damaged.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt makes things worse; the object takes additional damage or becomes more difficult to repair.

**Repair DC Examples:**
| Damage Type | Typical DC |
|-------------|------------|
| Minor damage (broken condition) | 15-20 |
| Moderate damage (damaged condition) | 20-25 |
| Severe damage (multiple conditions) | 25-30 |
| Catastrophic damage | 30+ |
