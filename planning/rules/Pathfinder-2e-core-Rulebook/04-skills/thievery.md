# Thievery (Dexterity)

Thievery measures your ability to steal, pick locks, disable devices, and perform other delicate manipulations.

## Untrained Actions

### Palm an Object [one-action] - STEALTH
You attempt to conceal a small object in your hand or on your person without being noticed. This is useful for picking up items surreptitiously or hiding objects you're carrying. Make a Thievery check against the Perception DC of observers who might notice you.

- **Critical Success:** You palm the object perfectly; even careful observers won't notice.
- **Success:** You conceal the object well; casual observers won't notice, but careful scrutiny might reveal it.
- **Failure:** Your attempt is noticed; observers see you concealing the object.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt is obvious and draws attention to what you're doing.

### Steal [one-action] - STEALTH
**Requirements:** You are adjacent to or in contact with the target.

You attempt to take an object from a creature without being noticed. This requires you to be close enough to reach the object (typically within 5 feet) and make a Thievery check against the target's Perception DC. The target might be aware of your presence but not necessarily watching your hands closely.

- **Critical Success:** You successfully take the object without the target noticing; you can even steal items from on their person if they're not being careful.
- **Success:** You take the object, but the target might notice something is missing after the fact.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the target notices what you're doing or you fail to get the object.
- **Critical Failure:** The target catches you in the act and might react aggressively.

## Trained Actions

### Disable a Device [two-actions] - MANIPULATE
**Requirements:** You have thieves' tools or appropriate implements.

You attempt to disable a mechanical device, typically a trap or security mechanism. This requires thieves' tools and takes at least 2 actions (more for complex devices). Make a Thievery check against the device's DC. The GM sets the DC based on the device's complexity and quality.

- **Critical Success:** You disable the device quickly and cleanly; you might also learn how it works or find ways to rearm it later.
- **Success:** You successfully disable the device, rendering it harmless.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the device remains active. You can try again but might trigger it in the process.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt triggers the device prematurely, potentially setting off traps or alarms.

**Disable Device DC Examples:**
| Device Type | Typical DC |
|-------------|------------|
| Simple lock or basic trap | 15-20 |
| Standard security mechanism | 20-25 |
| Complex lock or magical trap | 25-30 |
| Masterwork security system | 30+ |

### Pick a Lock [two-actions] - MANIPULATE
**Requirements:** You have thieves' tools.

You attempt to open a locked door, chest, or other container without the key. This requires thieves' tools and takes at least 2 actions (more for particularly difficult locks). Make a Thievery check against the lock's DC.

- **Critical Success:** You pick the lock quickly and silently; the lock remains undamaged and can be used normally afterward.
- **Success:** You successfully open the locked container or door.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the lock remains closed. You can try again but might damage the lock in the process.
- **Critical Failure:** Your tools break or jam the lock, making it more difficult to pick later (typically imposing a –5 penalty on future attempts).

**Pick Lock DC Examples:**
| Lock Type | Typical DC |
|-----------|------------|
| Simple padlock or chest lock | 15-20 |
| Standard door lock | 20-25 |
| Complex security lock | 25-30 |
| Masterwork or magical lock | 30+ |

## Notes on Thieves' Tools

Thieves' tools are essential for most Thievery tasks. They typically include:
- Lockpicks of various sizes and shapes
- Small prybars and tension wrenches
- Mirror on a flexible rod for seeing around corners
- Thin blades for manipulating mechanisms

Without thieves' tools, you take a –2 circumstance penalty to Thievery checks, and some tasks (like picking locks) might be impossible.
