# Bard

You are an inspirational performer and spellcaster, using music, words, and art to bolster allies and confound foes. Bards are versatile characters who excel at support, skill use, and magical versatility.

## Core Bard Traits

- **Primary Ability:** Charisma
- **Hit Point Die:** D8 per Bard level
- **Saving Throws:** Dexterity and Charisma

### Proficiencies
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Choose any 3 skills
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Choose 3 Musical Instruments
- **Armor Training:** Light armor

### Starting Equipment
Choose A or B:
- (A) Leather Armor, 2 Daggers, Musical Instrument of your choice, Entertainer's Pack, and 19 GP
- (B) 90 GP

## Class Features

### Level 1: Bardic Inspiration
You can supernaturally inspire others through words, music, or dance. As a Bonus Action, you can give one creature within 60 feet a Bardic Inspiration die (starting as a d6). Once within the next hour, when that creature fails a D20 Test, it can roll the Bardic Inspiration die and add the number rolled to the d20.

- You can confer Bardic Inspiration a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once)
- You regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest
- The die increases to d8 at level 5, d10 at level 10, and d12 at level 15

### Level 1: Spellcasting
You have learned to cast spells through your bardic arts. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for Bard spells. You can use a Musical Instrument as a Spellcasting Focus.

- **Cantrips:** You know two cantrips of your choice from the Bard spell list
- **Spells:** You prepare level 1+ spells from the Bard spell list
- **Spell Slots:** The Bard Features table shows how many spell slots you have

### Level 2: Expertise
You gain Expertise in two of your skill proficiencies of your choice. Performance and Persuasion are recommended if you have proficiency in them. At level 9, you gain Expertise in two more skills.

### Level 2: Jack of All Trades
You can add half your Proficiency Bonus (round down) to any ability check you make that uses a skill proficiency you lack and that doesn't otherwise use your Proficiency Bonus.

### Level 3: Bard Subclass
Choose a subclass (College). The College of Lore is detailed in the SRD.

### Level 5: Font of Inspiration
You now regain all your expended uses of Bardic Inspiration when you finish a Short or Long Rest. You can also expend a spell slot to regain one use of Bardic Inspiration.

### Level 7: Countercharm
You can disrupt mind-influencing effects. If you or a creature within 30 feet fails a saving throw against an effect that applies the Charmed or Frightened condition, you can take a Reaction to cause the save to be rerolled with Advantage.

### Level 10: Magical Secrets
Whenever you reach a Bard level and your Prepared Spells number increases, you can choose any of your new prepared spells from the Bard, Cleric, Druid, and Wizard spell lists. The chosen spells count as Bard spells for you.

### Level 14: Peerless Skill
When you make an ability check or attack roll and fail, you can expend one use of Bardic Inspiration; roll the die, and add the number rolled to the d20, potentially turning a failure into a success.

### Level 20: Words of Creation
You have power over all of Creation with the words of life and death. You always have the Power Word Kill and Power Word Heal spells prepared. When you cast either spell, you can target a second creature within 10 feet of the first target.

## College of Lore (Subclass)

Bards of the College of Lore collect spells and secrets from diverse sources, such as scholarly tomes, mystical rites, and peasant tales.

### Level 3: Cutting Words
When a creature that you can see within 60 feet makes a damage roll or succeeds on an ability check or attack roll, you can take a Reaction to expend one use of Bardic Inspiration; roll the die, and subtract the number rolled from the creature's roll.

### Level 3: Bonus Proficiencies
You gain proficiency with three skills of your choice.

### Level 6: Magical Discoveries
You learn two spells of your choice from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard spell lists. You always have these spells prepared.
