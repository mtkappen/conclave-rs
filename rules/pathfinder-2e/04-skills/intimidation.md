# Intimidation (Charisma)

Intimidation measures your ability to coerce, threaten, and demoralize others.

## Untrained Actions

### Coerce - EXPLORATION
You attempt to force a creature to do something through threats or intimidation. This typically requires you to have some means of threatening the target (a weapon, allies, magical power, etc.). The GM sets the DC based on how reasonable the demand is and the target's disposition toward you.

- **Critical Success:** The target complies with your demands and may continue to cooperate for a reasonable time.
- **Success:** The target complies with your immediate demands but remains hostile.
- **Failure:** The target refuses your demands.
- **Critical Failure:** The target becomes enraged or calls for help, potentially leading to combat.

## Trained Actions

### Demoralize [one-action] - AUDIBLE MANIPULATE VISUAL
**Requirements:** You can see and communicate with the target, and the target can perceive you.

You attempt to frighten a creature using threats, displays of power, or other intimidating tactics. Make an Intimidation check against the target's Will DC.

- **Critical Success:** The target becomes frightened 3.
- **Success:** The target becomes frightened 1.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the target is unmoved.
- **Critical Failure:** The target becomes immune to your Demoralize attempts for 24 hours, and its attitude toward you worsens by one step.

A creature that is frightened from your Demoralize attempt becomes immune to being demoralized by you for 1 minute. You can't Demoralize a creature that doesn't understand your language or can't see or hear you.
