# Running Monsters

This section provides guidance for Game Masters on how to effectively use monsters in encounters, both combat and non-combat situations.

## Encounter Design

### Building Encounters

When designing encounters, consider:

1. **Party Size:** More characters mean more actions per round
2. **Party Level:** Higher-level parties can handle tougher threats
3. **Monster CR:** Use Challenge Rating as a starting point for difficulty
4. **Environment:** Terrain and conditions affect combat dynamics
5. **Objectives:** Combat isn't always about killing everything

### Difficulty Levels

- **Easy:** Minimal risk, good for introducing new mechanics
- **Medium:** Moderate challenge, requires tactical thinking
- **Hard:** Significant threat, may require resources to overcome
- **Deadly:** High risk of character death or serious injury

## Monster Tactics

### Understanding Monster Behavior

Monsters should act according to their intelligence and nature:
- **Low Intelligence (Int 1–4):** Act on instinct, simple tactics
- **Average Intelligence (Int 5–8):** Basic strategy, follow orders
- **High Intelligence (Int 9+):** Complex tactics, use environment

### Combat Tactics

#### Melee Creatures
- Focus on vulnerable targets (spellcasters, ranged attackers)
- Use terrain for cover when possible
- Flank enemies to gain Advantage
- Retreat when outnumbered or wounded

#### Ranged Creatures
- Maintain distance from melee combatants
- Use cover and positioning
- Target high-value enemies first
- Move between shots if threatened

#### Spellcasting Creatures
- Cast control spells early in combat
- Protect themselves with defensive magic
- Focus on area denial
- Save powerful spells for critical moments

### Group Tactics

Monsters working together should:
- Coordinate attacks to overwhelm targets
- Use different abilities synergistically
- Protect weaker allies
- Fall back when the tide turns against them

## Non-Combat Encounters

### Social Interactions

Many monsters can be negotiated with:
- Understand their motivations and goals
- Offer something they want
- Respect their culture and customs
- Know when to fight vs. when to talk

### Exploration Challenges

Monsters in exploration contexts:
- Patrol their territory
- Investigate unusual sounds or smells
- Set traps or ambushes
- Flee if outmatched

## Legendary Creatures

Legendary creatures are powerful foes that can challenge entire parties. They have special rules:

### Legendary Actions
These allow legendary creatures to take extra actions outside their turn:
- Typically 3 legendary actions per round
- Can only be used at the end of another creature's turn
- Each legendary action has a cost (1–3 actions)
- Regain all spent legendary actions at the start of their turn

### Lair Actions
Powerful creatures in their home territory can take lair actions:
- Occur on initiative count 20
- Don't use legendary action slots
- Often affect the environment or summon minions
- End when the creature leaves its lair

## Monster Variations

### Young vs. Adult vs. Ancient

Many monster types have age categories:
- **Young:** Weaker, less experienced (lower CR)
- **Adult:** Standard version of the monster
- **Ancient:** Powerful, experienced versions (higher CR)

### Elite Variants
Some monsters can be upgraded for tougher encounters:
- Add more Hit Points
- Increase damage output
- Give additional actions or abilities
- Add legendary actions for boss fights

## Environmental Interactions

Monsters interact with their environment:
- **Cover:** Use walls, trees, and obstacles for protection
- **Difficult Terrain:** Some creatures ignore it (flying, burrowing)
- **Lighting:** Darkvision gives advantages in Darkness
- **Hazards:** Traps, fire, falling rocks can affect both sides

## Morale

Not all monsters fight to the death:
- **Intelligent creatures** may surrender if outmatched
- **Brutal creatures** might kill instead of capturing
- **Mercenary creatures** flee when payment ends
- **Territorial creatures** defend their home fiercely

### When Monsters Flee
Consider having monsters retreat when:
- Half or more are defeated
- Their leader is killed
- They're significantly outmatched
- Their goal is achieved (or impossible)

## Minions and Swarms

Some encounters feature many weak creatures:
- **Minions:** Have 1 HP but deal normal damage
- **Swarm Creatures:** Represent groups as single entities
- **Horde Tactics:** Overwhelm with numbers, use pack tactics

## Boss Encounters

For memorable boss fights:
- Give the creature legendary actions
- Add minions to protect the boss
- Use a dramatic environment
- Include environmental hazards or objectives
- Consider multiple phases for very powerful foes
