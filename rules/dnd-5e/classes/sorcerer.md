# Sorcerer

You are a spellcaster whose magic comes from within, born with innate magical ability from your bloodline or exposure to mystical forces. Sorcerers rely on Charisma and can manipulate their spells through Metamagic.

## Core Sorcerer Traits

- **Primary Ability:** Charisma
- **Hit Point Die:** D6 per Sorcerer level
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution and Charisma

### Proficiencies
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Choose 2: Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, or Religion
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** Daggers, darts, slings, crossbows (light), quarterstaffs
- **Armor Training:** None

### Starting Equipment
Choose A or B:
- (A) Light crossbow and 20 bolts or any simple weapon
- (B) Component pouch or Arcane focus
- Choose: Dungeoneer's pack or Explorer's pack
- Two Daggers

## Class Features

### Level 1: Spellcasting
Magic is the essence of your being. You know a number of cantrips and spells that you cast using Charisma as your spellcasting ability.

- **Cantrips:** You know four cantrips of your choice from the Sorcerer spell list. You learn additional cantrips at higher levels
- **Spells Known:** You know two 1st-level spells of your choice from the Sorcerer spell list. The Spells Known column shows how many spells you know at each level
- **Spell Slots:** The Sorcerer Features table shows how many spell slots you have
- **Spellcasting Ability:** Charisma is your spellcasting ability. Your spell save DC = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Charisma modifier; Spell attack bonus = Proficiency Bonus + Charisma modifier

### Level 1: Sorcerous Origin
Choose a sorcerous origin that describes the source of your innate magical power (Draconic Bloodline is detailed in the SRD). Your choice grants you features at 1st level and again at 6th, 14th, and 18th level.

### Level 2: Font of Magic
At 2nd level, you tap into a deep wellspring of magic within yourself. This wellspring is represented by sorcery points, which allow you to create a variety of magical effects. You have two sorcery points, and you gain more as you reach higher levels (up to 20 at level 20). You can never have more sorcery points than shown for your level. You regain all spent sorcery points when you finish a Long Rest.

#### Create Spell Slots
As a Bonus Action, you can create one or more spell slots from your sorcery points. Creating a spell slot costs sorcery points equal to the slot's level × 2 (e.g., creating a 3rd-level slot costs 6 points). You can't create spell slots higher than 5th level.

#### Convert Spell Slots
As a Bonus Action, you can transform one or more expended spell slots into sorcery points. A 1st-level slot gives 2 points, a 2nd-level slot gives 3 points, etc., up to a 5th-level slot which gives 7 points.

### Level 2: Metamagic
At 2nd level, you gain the ability to twist your spells to suit your needs. You gain two of the following Metamagic options of your choice (Twin Spell and Quickened Spell are recommended). You gain more at levels 3, 10, and 17. You can use only one Metamagic option on a spell when you cast it, unless noted otherwise.

#### Common Metamagic Options:
- **Careful Spell:** When casting an area effect spell, choose a number of creatures up to your Charisma modifier who automatically succeed on their saving throws
- **Distant Spell:** Double the range of spells with a range (or make touch spells 30 feet)
- **Empowered Spell:** Reroll up to your Charisma modifier damage dice and use the new rolls
- **Extended Spell:** Double a spell's duration (up to 24 hours for spells lasting 1 minute or longer)
- **Heightened Spell:** Give a target Disadvantage on one saving throw against your spell
- **Quickened Spell:** Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action as a Bonus Action (costs 2 sorcery points)
- **Subtle Spell:** Cast without Verbal or Somatic components (costs 1 sorcery point)
- **Twin Spell:** Target a second creature with the same spell if it targets only one creature and doesn't have a range of Self (costs sorcery points equal to spell level, minimum 1)

### Level 3: Sorcerous Restoration
At 3rd level, you regain 4 sorcery points whenever you finish a Short Rest.

## Draconic Bloodline (Subclass)

Your innate magic comes from draconic magic that was mingled with your blood or that of your ancestors. Most often, sorcerers with this origin trace their descent back to a mighty sorcerer of ancient times who made a bargain with a dragon or who might even have claimed a dragon parent.

### Level 1: Dragon Ancestor
Choose one type of dragon (Black, Blue, Brass, Bronze, Copper, Gold, Green, Red, Silver, or White). You know the Draconic language and one cantrip of your choice from the Sorcerer spell list. Your chosen dragon's damage type is associated with your magic (Acid for Black/Copper, Lightning for Blue/Brass, Fire for Brass/Red, Cold for White/Silver, Poison for Green/Gold).

### Level 1: Draconic Resilience
Your skin resembles a dragon's scales. When you aren't wearing armor, your base AC equals 13 + your Dexterity modifier. You can use a Shield and still gain this benefit. In addition, your Hit Point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level in this class.

### Level 6: Elemental Affinity
When you cast a spell that deals damage of the type associated with your dragon ancestor, you can add your Charisma modifier to one damage roll of that spell. At the same time, you can spend 1 sorcery point to gain Resistance to that damage type for 1 hour.

### Level 14: Dragon Wings
You gain the ability to sprout a pair of dragon wings from your back, gaining a flying speed equal to your current speed. You can create these wings as a Bonus Action on your turn. They last until you dismiss them as a Bonus Action on your turn. You can't manifest your wings while wearing armor unless the armor is made to accommodate them, and clothing not made to accommodate your wings must be destroyed when you manifest them.

### Level 18: Draconic Presence
You can channel the dread presence of your dragon ancestor, causing those around you to be awestruck or frightened. As an action, you can spend 5 sorcery points to draw on this power and exude a protective aura in a 60-foot radius centered on yourself. For 1 minute or until you have the Incapacitated condition, creatures of your choice in that area must make Wisdom saving throws. On a failed save, a creature has the Frightened condition for the duration. On a successful save, the creature is immune to this effect for 24 hours.
