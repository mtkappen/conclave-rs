# Chapter 8: The Age of Lost Omens

## Introduction

The Age of Lost Omens is the current era in the Pathfinder campaign setting, beginning when the god Aroden died in 4606 AR (Absalom Reckoning). This age is characterized by uncertainty and opportunity—a time in need of heroes like never before.

## History: Earthfall to the Present

### Earthfall
Ten thousand years ago, the world of Golarion came close to ending. Earthfall, as this extinction-level event came to be known, saw the world pummeled by a shower of falling stars that sank continents, hollowed out new seas, and destroyed civilizations. It took centuries for the world to recover, and centuries more for society to rebuild.

### Recovery and Rebuilding
- **Dwarves** ascended to the surface from the underground reaches of the Darklands in their legendary Quest for Sky
- **Elves** returned from the neighboring planet of Castrovel via a network of portals to reclaim their lands and traditions
- **Gnomes** from the mysterious First World sought shelter from a now-forgotten terror
- Survivors from other ancestries emerged during the Age of Darkness to reclaim their ancestral lands

### The Age of Destiny
Less than 2,000 years after Earthfall, humanity built wondrous structures and relearned magic. Among these human nations walked **Aroden**—an immortal survivor of Earthfall who discovered the Starstone, a fragment from the stars that fell during Earthfall.

Contact with the Starstone assailed Aroden with visions, subjected him to deadly martial trials, and posed exhausting moral quandaries. He emerged as a living god, and his first divine act was to raise the Starstone and the mass of land on which it lay from the bottom of the Inner Sea to form the **Isle of Kortos** (Starstone Isle), where he established the city of **Absalom**.

### The Death of Aroden
In the centuries following, Absalom grew into one of the largest cities in the world. Prophecy spoke of Aroden's return to lead humanity into an Age of Glory. But instead, Aroden died, and with him the reliability of prophecy as well. Golarion was wracked by storms, war, and supernatural devastation.

This marked the beginning of the **Age of Lost Omens**—an age in need of heroes like never before.

## The World of Golarion

### Continents
Golarion consists of several major continents:

- **Avistan** - Northern continent containing the Inner Sea region
- **Garund** - Southern continent, extends further south from the Inner Sea
- **Casmaron** - Largest continent, home to the Castrovin Sea and ancient empires
- **Tian Xia** - Eastern continent, often called the Dragon Empires
- **Crown of the World** - Frozen northern reach connecting Tian Xia and Avistan
- **Sarusan** - Mysterious southern continent shrouded by storms and vexing currents
- **Arcadia** - Western land with unusual magical traditions

### The Darklands
Beneath all these lands lie the twisting tunnels of the tripartite underground realm known collectively as the Darklands, which houses great horrors and dangers but equally great opportunities for triumph and treasure.

### Lost Azlant
Heading west from the Inner Sea region, sailors encounter the shattered remnants of lost Azlant, a ruined continent that hosted one of humanity's first empires until it was destroyed during Earthfall.

## Golarion in the Solar System

Golarion is one of 11 worlds that orbit its sun:
- **Akiton** - Close neighbor, red planet
- **Castrovel** - Home of the elves
- **Aucturn** - Farthest planet, has influenced Golarion with alien inhabitants

## The Great Beyond

Countless planets lie beyond Golarion's solar system in the Material Plane. Other planes of existence and strange dimensions wrap this reality in layered, nested spheres known collectively as the Great Beyond.

### Inner Sphere
The core of the multiverse contains:
- **Material Plane** - Golarion and countless other worlds
- **Elemental Planes** - Air, Water, Earth, Fire (surrounding the Material Plane)
- **Positive Energy Plane** - Node of life and creation
- **Negative Energy Plane** - Node of death and destruction

### Outer Sphere
Where gods dwell and souls of the dead gather to be judged by Pharasma atop the spire in her Boneyard. Realms include:
- Heaven
- The Abyss (demon-held)
- Hell
- Elysium

The Outer Sphere's realms are manifestations of philosophies and belief, representing all of reality.

### Other Planes and Dimensions
- **Ethereal Plane** - Ghost world overlaying the Inner Sphere
- **Astral Plane** - Fills the gulf between Inner Sphere and Outer Sphere
- **First World** - Fey-haunted dimension
- **Shadow Plane** - Sinister dimension overlaying the Material Plane
- **Dreamlands** - Visited often (if accidentally)
- **Dimension of Time** - Rarely visited

### Time
Like Earth, Golarion spins on its axis once roughly every 24 hours. A week has 7 days and a year has 52 weeks. An extra leap day is added to the second month every 4 years to keep the calendar synchronized with the astronomical year.
