# Survival (Wisdom)

Survival measures your ability to navigate and endure in the wilderness, including tracking, foraging, and reading natural signs.

## Untrained Actions

### Sense Direction - EXPLORATION
You attempt to determine which direction you're facing or find your way to a known location. This is particularly useful when traveling without clear landmarks or in featureless terrain. The GM sets the DC based on the difficulty of the terrain and how well you know the area.

- **Critical Success:** You correctly identify your direction and can navigate efficiently toward your destination; you might also notice useful landmarks or resources along the way.
- **Success:** You correctly identify your general direction and can navigate without getting lost.
- **Failure:** You're uncertain of your direction and might wander off course.
- **Critical Failure:** You become disoriented and head in the wrong direction.

### Subsist - DOWNTIME
If you need to provide food and shelter, you can use the Subsist downtime activity. This typically uses Survival if you're in the wild (as opposed to Society for settlements).

You attempt to provide food and shelter for yourself, and possibly others as well, with a standard of living described on page 294. The GM determines the DC based on the nature of the place where you're trying to Subsist. You might need a minimum proficiency rank to Subsist in particularly strange environments.

Unlike most downtime activities, you can Subsist after 8 hours or less of exploration, but if you do, you take a –5 penalty.

- **Critical Success:** You either provide a subsistence living for yourself and one additional creature, or you improve your own food and shelter, granting yourself a comfortable living.
- **Success:** You find enough food and shelter with basic protection from the elements to provide you a subsistence living.
- **Failure:** You're exposed to the elements and don't get enough food, becoming fatigued until you attain sufficient food and shelter.
- **Critical Failure:** You attract trouble, eat something you shouldn't, or otherwise worsen your situation. You take a –2 circumstance penalty to checks to Subsist for 1 week.

**Sample Subsist Tasks (Wilderness):**
| Task | Proficiency |
|------|-------------|
| Lush forest with calm weather | Untrained |
| Typical hillside or village | Trained |
| Typical mountains or insular hamlet | Expert |
| Typical desert or city under siege | Master |
| Barren wasteland or city of undead | Legendary |

## Trained Actions

### Cover Tracks - EXPLORATION
You attempt to hide the fact that you (and possibly your companions) have passed through an area. This takes 10 minutes per mile of terrain you're covering tracks for. Make a Survival check against the Perception DC of anyone who might track you. The GM may adjust the DC based on terrain type and weather conditions.

- **Critical Success:** Your tracks are virtually impossible to follow; trackers take a –10 penalty to Track checks against you.
- **Success:** Your tracks are difficult to follow; trackers take a –5 penalty to Track checks against you.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; your tracks remain visible and easy to follow.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt leaves obvious signs that draw attention to your passage; trackers gain a +2 bonus to Track checks against you.

### Track - EXPLORATION
You attempt to follow the trail left by a creature or group of creatures. This requires suitable tracks (not washed away by rain, covered by snow, etc.) and takes 10 minutes per mile of tracking. Make a Survival check against a DC set by the GM based on the age of the tracks, terrain type, and other factors.

- **Critical Success:** You follow the trail accurately and quickly; you might also gather additional information about the tracked creature(s), such as their number, direction, or condition.
- **Success:** You successfully follow the trail without losing it.
- **Failure:** You lose the trail and must search for it again (taking additional time).
- **Critical Failure:** You follow the wrong trail or become disoriented; you might waste significant time or end up in a dangerous location.

**Track DC Examples:**
| Track Condition | Typical DC |
|-----------------|------------|
| Fresh tracks on soft ground | 10-15 |
| Several hours old, normal terrain | 15-20 |
| Old tracks, difficult terrain | 20-25 |
| Very old tracks, harsh conditions | 25+ |
