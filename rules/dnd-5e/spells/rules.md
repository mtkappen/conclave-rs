# Spellcasting Rules

Spells are magical effects that characters can cast using their innate power or through study and devotion. This section covers the general rules for spellcasting that apply to all spellcasting classes.

## Spell Levels

Every spell has a level from 0 to 9. A spell's level is a general indicator of how powerful it is. Cantrips are spells of level 0 that can be cast at will without using a spell slot.

- **Cantrips (Level 0):** Simple spells learned through practice
- **1st–9th Level:** More powerful spells requiring spell slots

## Spell Slots

Spellcasters have a limited number of spell slots, which represent their capacity to cast spells. Each spell slot has a level (1st through 9th), and you can use a slot of that level or higher to cast a spell.

- **Casting at Higher Levels:** Some spells become more powerful when cast using a higher-level spell slot
- **Slot Recovery:** Different classes recover spell slots in different ways (Long Rest, Short Rest, Arcane Recovery, etc.)

## Casting Time

Spells have specific casting times that determine how long it takes to cast them:

### Action
Most spells require a standard action to cast. You can cast these spells as part of your normal turn in combat.

### Bonus Action
Some spells can be cast as a Bonus Action. You can only cast one Bonus Action spell per turn, and if you cast a Bonus Action spell, the only other spell you can cast that turn is a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action.

### Reaction
These spells are cast in response to specific triggers (like Counterspell or Shield).

### Longer Casting Times
Some spells require minutes, hours, or even days to cast. These are typically ritual spells or powerful magic.

## Range

The range of a spell determines how far away its target can be:

- **Self:** The spell affects only you
- **Touch:** You must touch the target
- **Specific distances:** Measured in feet (30 ft., 60 ft., 120 ft., etc.)
- **Unlimited:** The spell can reach anywhere on the same plane of existence

## Components

Spells require specific components to be cast:

### Verbal (V)
The spell requires verbal incantations. You must be able to speak clearly and loudly enough to be heard. Creatures that can't speak or are silenced can't cast spells with verbal components.

### Somatic (S)
The spell requires hand gestures. You must have free use of at least one hand to perform these gestures. If your hands are bound or you're holding something in both hands, you may not be able to cast somatic spells.

### Material (M)
The spell requires specific material components. Some materials have a gold cost specified and are consumed when the spell is cast. Others are not consumed and can be reused. A component pouch or spellcasting focus can often substitute for material components that don't have a cost.

## Duration

Spells have durations that determine how long their effects last:

### Instantaneous
The spell's effect occurs immediately and doesn't persist.

### Concentration
Some spells require you to maintain concentration to keep them active. You can concentrate on only one spell at a time. Taking damage, being incapacitated, or casting another concentration spell ends your current concentration.

### Fixed Duration
The spell lasts for a specific amount of time (1 round, 1 minute, 1 hour, etc.).

### Until Dispelled or Triggered
Some spells last until they are dispelled or until a specific trigger occurs.

## Saving Throws and Attack Rolls

### Spell Save DC
When a spell requires a saving throw, the DC is calculated as:
**Spell Save DC = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier**

### Spell Attack Roll
Some spells require you to make a spell attack roll:
**Spell Attack Bonus = Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier**

If the attack roll equals or exceeds the target's AC, the spell hits.

## Ritual Casting

Some spells have the ritual tag. These spells can be cast without expending a spell slot if you take an additional 10 minutes to cast them as a ritual. Not all classes can cast rituals; some require the Ritual Caster feat or specific class features.

## Spellcasting Ability

Each spellcasting class uses a different ability score for their spells:
- **Intelligence:** Wizards, Artificers
- **Wisdom:** Clerics, Druids, Rangers, Monks (some)
- **Charisma:** Sorcerers, Warlocks, Bards, Paladins

## Counterspells

You can use the Counterspell to interrupt another creature in the process of casting a spell. If the creature is casting a spell of 3rd level or lower, its spell fails and has no effect. If it's casting a spell of 4th level or higher, make an ability check using your spellcasting ability to see if the spell is countered.

## Dispel Magic

You can end ongoing spells using Dispel Magic. The spell automatically ends spells of 3rd level or lower. For higher-level spells, make an ability check using your spellcasting ability.
