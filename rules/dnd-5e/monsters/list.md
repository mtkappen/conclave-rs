# Monster List Overview

This section provides an overview of the monsters available in the D&D 5e SRD, organized by type and challenge rating for easy reference.

## Monster Types

### Aberrations
Bizarre, alien creatures from other dimensions or twisted by dark forces.
- **Examples:** Aboleth, Beholder, Mind Flayer, Cloaker
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/4 to 10+

### Animals
Non-humanoid creatures of the natural world.
- **Examples:** Bear (Black, Brown, Polar), Wolf, Eagle, Shark, Snake
- **Typical CR Range:** 0 to 5

### Celestials
Inhabitants of the upper planes, typically good-aligned.
- **Examples:** Angel (Devil, Planetar, Solar), Pegasus, Unicorn
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/2 to 21+

### Constructs
Artificial creatures brought to life through magic or technology.
- **Examples:** Animated Armor, Golem (Clay, Flesh, Iron, Stone), Shield Guardian
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/2 to 9

### Dragons
Powerful reptilian creatures with magical abilities.
- **Examples:** Dragon (Black, Blue, Brass, Bronze, Copper, Gold, Green, Red, Silver, White) - Wyrmling through Ancient
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/4 to 24+

### Elementals
Creatures composed of pure elemental energy.
- **Examples:** Air Elemental, Earth Elemental, Fire Elemental, Water Elemental, Mephit (various types)
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/4 to 8

### Fey
Magical creatures from the Feywild, often whimsical or dangerous.
- **Examples:** Dryad, Pixie, Satyr, Sprite, Redcap
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/8 to 9

### Fiends
Evil inhabitants of the lower planes (demons, devils, etc.)
- **Examples:** Devil (Barbed, Bone, Ice), Demon (Balor, Marilith, Quasit), Imp, Pit Fiend
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/8 to 20+

### Giants
Huge humanoid creatures with immense strength.
- **Examples:** Giant (Hill, Mountain, Frost, Fire, Stone, Cloud, Storm)
- **Typical CR Range:** 5 to 13

### Humanoids
Sentient beings similar to player character races.
- **Examples:** Goblin, Hobgoblin, Orc, Bugbear, Kobold, Human variants (Soldier, Noble, Acolyte)
- **Typical CR Range:** 0 to 5

### Monstrosities
Monstrous beasts that don't fit other categories.
- **Examples:** Behir, Chimera, Displacer Beast, Griffon, Owlbear, Rust Monster
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/8 to 13+

### Oozes
Amorphous creatures, often dangerous or mindless.
- **Examples:** Gelatinous Cube, Gray Ooze, Black Pudding
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/4 to 6

### Plants
Living vegetation and plant-based creatures.
- **Examples:** Shambling Mound, Treant, Violet Fungus, Myconid
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/2 to 9

### Undead
Once-living creatures animated by spiritual or supernatural forces.
- **Examples:** Zombie, Skeleton, Ghost, Wight, Lich, Vampire, Mummy (Lord)
- **Typical CR Range:** 1/8 to 21+

## Challenge Rating Quick Reference

### CR 0–1/4 (Trivial Threats)
- Commoner, Goblin, Kobold, Rat, Spider, Bat, Snake
- Suitable for level 1 parties in groups of 3–5

### CR 1/2–2 (Minor Threats)
- Bugbear, Ogre, Wolf, Giant Badger, Black Bear
- Suitable for level 1–4 parties

### CR 3–5 (Moderate Threats)
- Troll, Ettin, Minotaur, Hydra (young), Young Dragon
- Suitable for level 5–8 parties

### CR 6–10 (Significant Threats)
- Ancient Dragon (young/adult), Lich, Beholder, Mind Flayer
- Suitable for level 9–14 parties

### CR 11+ (Major Threats)
- Tarrasque, Ancient Dragons, Demogorgon, Archmage
- Suitable for level 15+ parties

## Creature Categories by Environment

### Underground/Dungeon
- Beholder, Cloaker, Gelatinous Cube, Mind Flayer, Ooze variants
- Drow, Duergar, Goblin, Hobgoblin, Kobold
- Skeleton, Zombie, Ghost, Wight

### Forest/Wilderness
- Bear variants, Wolf, Owlbear, Displacer Beast
- Dryad, Satyr, Treant, Shambling Mound
- Elf, Human Ranger, Centaur

### Mountain/High Altitude
- Giant variants (Hill, Stone, Cloud)
- Griffon, Pegasus, Roc
- Dragon variants (Blue, Red, White)

### Aquatic
- Shark variants, Kraken, Sea Hag
- Merfolk, Sahuagin
- Water Elemental, Triton

### Urban/Settlements
- Human variants (Noble, Soldier, Criminal, Acolyte)
- Guard, Knight, Spy
- Imp, Quasit (familiar)

## Special Monster Categories

### Dragons by Color
- **Chromatic (Evil):** Black (Acid), Blue (Lightning), Green (Poison), Red (Fire), White (Cold)
- **Metallic (Good):** Brass (Fire), Bronze (Lightning), Copper (Acid), Gold (Fire), Silver (Cold)

### Devils vs Demons
- **Devils (Lawful Evil):** Barbed, Bone, Ice, Chain, Imp, Pit Fiend
- **Demons (Chaotic Evil):** Balor, Marilith, Quasit, Hezrou, Nalfeshnee

### Liches and Undead Lords
- Lich, Mummy Lord, Vampire (Spawn), Death Knight

## Using the Monster List

When selecting monsters for your campaign:
1. **Match CR to party level** for appropriate challenge
2. **Consider variety** in monster types and abilities
3. **Think about environment** and where creatures live
4. **Mix combat styles** (melee, ranged, spellcasters)
5. **Include social options** when possible (intelligent monsters)
