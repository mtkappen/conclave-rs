# Sorcerer

## Introduction

You didn't choose to become a spellcaster—you were born one. There's magic in your blood, whether a divinity touched one of your ancestors, a forebear communed with a primal creature, or a powerful occult ritual influenced your line. Self-reflection and study allow you to refine your inherent magical skills and unlock new, more powerful abilities. The power in your blood carries a risk, however, and you constantly face the choice of whether you'll rise to become a master spellcaster or fall into destruction.

### During combat encounters...
You use spells to injure your enemies, influence their minds, and hamper their movements. You might be too vulnerable to get into melee combat, or your bloodline might give you abilities that help you hold your own in a brawl. While your magic is powerful, to conserve your best spells—or when you've used them all up—you also rely on cantrips.

### During social encounters...
Your natural charisma makes you good at interacting with people.

### While exploring...
You detect the magic around you, finding treasures and warning your adventuring group of magical traps. When the group encounters mysteries or problems related to your bloodline, you try to solve them.

### In downtime...
You craft magic items or scribe scrolls. Your bloodline might drive you to research your ancestry or associate with affiliated people or creatures.

### You Might...
- Have a strong independent streak, and whether you embrace or reject your magical heritage, you long to distinguish yourself both as a spellcaster and as an individual.
- View your lineage with fascination, fear, or something in between—anything from wholehearted acceptance to vehement rejection.
- Rely on magic items, such as scrolls and wands, to supplement your limited selection of spells.

### Others Probably...
- Marvel at your ability to create magic from thin air and view your abilities with equal parts admiration and mistrust.
- Consider you less dedicated than studious wizards, devoted clerics, and other practitioners of magic, since power comes to you naturally.
- Assume you're as unpredictable as the magic you bring forth, even if your personality proves otherwise.

## Class Features

You gain these abilities as a sorcerer. Abilities gained at higher levels list the requisite levels next to their names.

### Ancestry and Background (1st)
In addition to the abilities provided by your class at 1st level, you have the benefits of your selected ancestry and background, as described in Chapter 2.

### Bloodline (1st)
Choose a bloodline that gives you your spellcasting talent. This choice determines the type of spells you cast and the spell list you choose them from, additional spells you learn, and additional trained skills. You also gain Focus Points and special focus spells based on your bloodline.

**Bloodlines:**
- **Aberrant:** Strange and unknowable influence gives occult spells. Skills: Occultism, Society.
- **Angelic:** Holy grace bestows divine spells. Skills: Diplomacy, Religion.
- **Demonic:** Sinful corruption gives divine spells. Skills: Intimidation, Religion.
- **Diabolic:** Bond with devils gives divine spells. Skills: Diplomacy, Intimidation.
- **Draconic:** Blood of dragons grants arcane spells. Skills: Arcana, Nature.
- **Elemental:** Power of elements manifests as primal spells. Skills: Intimidation, Nature.
- **Fey:** Influence from the fey gives primal spells. Skills: Deception, Nature.
- **Hag:** Blight of a hag has given you occult spells. Skills: Deception, Occultism.
- **Imperial:** Ancient power grants arcane spells. Skills: Arcana, Society.
- **Undead:** Touch of death gives divine spells. Skills: Intimidation, Religion.

### Sorcerer Spellcasting (1st)
Your bloodline provides you with incredible magical power. You can cast spells using the Cast a Spell activity, supplying material, somatic, and verbal components. As a sorcerer, you can usually replace material components with somatic components, so you don't need a spell component pouch.

Each day, you can cast up to three 1st-level spells. You must know spells to cast them, learned via the spell repertoire class feature. The number of spells you can cast each day is called your spell slots.

Some spells require a spell attack roll or enemies roll against your spell DC (typically saving throw). Your spell attack rolls and spell DCs use your Charisma modifier.

### Heightening Spells
When you get spell slots of 2nd level and higher, you can fill those slots with stronger versions of lower-level spells. This increases the spell's level to match the spell slot. You must have a spell in your spell repertoire at the level you want to cast in order to heighten it to that level. Many spells have specific improvements when heightened to certain levels.

### Cantrips
A cantrip is a special type of spell that doesn't use spell slots. You can cast a cantrip at will, any number of times per day. A cantrip is always automatically heightened to half your level rounded up—usually equal to the highest level of spell you can cast as a sorcerer.

### Spell Repertoire (1st)
The collection of spells you can cast is called your spell repertoire. At 1st level, you learn two 1st-level spells of your choice and four cantrips of your choice, plus an additional spell and cantrip from your bloodline. You choose these from common spells from the tradition corresponding to your bloodline, or from other spells from that tradition to which you have access.

You add to this spell repertoire as you increase in level. Each time you get a spell slot, you add a spell of the same level to your spell repertoire. When you gain access to a new level of spells, your first new spell is always your bloodline spell.

### Swapping Spells in Your Repertoire
Each time you gain a level and learn new spells, you can swap out one of your old spells for a different spell of the same level. This spell can be a cantrip, but you can't swap out bloodline spells. You can also swap out spells by retraining during downtime.

### Skill Feats (2nd)
At 2nd level and every 2 levels thereafter, you gain a skill feat from Chapter 5. You must be trained or better in the corresponding skill to select a skill feat.

### Sorcerer Feats (2nd)
At 2nd level and every even-numbered level, you gain a sorcerer class feat.

### General Feats (3rd)
At 3rd level and every 4 levels thereafter, you gain a general feat from Chapter 5.

### Signature Spells (3rd)
You've learned to cast some of your spells more flexibly. For each spell level you have access to, choose one spell of that level to be a signature spell. You don't need to learn heightened versions of signature spells separately; instead, you can heighten these spells freely. If you've learned a signature spell at a higher level than its minimum, you can also cast all its lower-level versions without learning those separately.

### Skill Increases (3rd)
At 3rd level and every 2 levels thereafter, you gain a skill increase. Use it to become trained in one skill or increase your proficiency rank in one skill you're already trained in to expert. At 7th level you can become master, and at 15th level legendary.

### Ability Boosts (5th)
At 5th level and every 5 levels thereafter, you boost four different ability scores.

### Ancestry Feats (5th)
In addition to the ancestry feat you started with, you gain an ancestry feat at 5th level and every 4 levels thereafter.

### Magical Fortitude (5th)
Magical power has improved your body's resiliency. Your proficiency rank for Fortitude saves increases to expert.

### Expert Spellcaster (7th)
Your inherent magic responds easily and powerfully to your command. Your proficiency ranks for spell attack rolls and spell DCs for spells of your bloodline's tradition increase to expert.

### Lightning Reflexes (9th)
Your reflexes are lightning fast. Your proficiency rank for Reflex saves increases to expert.

### Alertness (11th)
You remain alert to threats around you. Your proficiency rank for Perception increases to expert.

### Simple Weapon Expertise (11th)
Training and magic improved your weapon technique. Your proficiency rank for simple weapons increases to expert.

### Defensive Robes (13th)
The flow of magic and your defensive training combine to help you get out of the way before an attack. Your proficiency rank in unarmored defense increases to expert.

### Weapon Specialization (13th)
You've learned to inflict grave wounds with your practiced weapons. You deal 2 additional damage with weapons and unarmed attacks in which you are an expert. This damage increases to 3 if you're a master, and 4 if you're legendary.

### Master Spellcaster (15th)
You have achieved mastery over the magic in your blood. Your proficiency ranks for spell attack rolls and spell DCs for spells of your bloodline's tradition increase to master.

### Bloodline Paragon (19th)
You have perfected the magic in your bloodline. Add two common 10th-level spells of your tradition to your repertoire. You gain a single 10th-level spell slot you can use to cast these spells, using sorcerer spellcasting. Unlike other spell slots, you don't gain more 10th-level spells as you level up, and they can't be used for abilities that let you cast spells without expending spell slots.

### Legendary Spellcaster (19th)
You demonstrate prodigious talent for spellcasting. Your proficiency ranks for spell attack rolls and spell DCs for spells of your bloodline's tradition increase to legendary.

## Bloodlines

### Aberrant
A strange and unknowable influence gives you occult spells.
- **Spell List:** Occult
- **Bloodline Skills:** Occultism, Society
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: daze; 1st: detect thoughts; 2nd: mind link; 3rd: clairaudience; 4th: confusion; 5th: telekinesis; 6th: heroism; 7th: mind trap; 8th: dominate; 9th: mind blank
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: alien memories; advanced: aberrant form; greater: mind shatter
- **Blood Magic:** Alien knowledge grants you or one target a +1 status bonus to Perception checks for 1 round.

### Angelic
Holy grace bestows divine spells upon you.
- **Spell List:** Divine
- **Bloodline Skills:** Diplomacy, Religion
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: light; 1st: bless; 2nd: shield of faith; 3rd: daylight; 4th: divine wrath; 5th: flame strike; 6th: blade barrier; 7th: divine decree; 8th: divine aura; 9th: foresight
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: angelic halo; advanced: angelic wings; greater: celestial brand
- **Blood Magic:** An angelic aura protects you or one target, granting a +1 status bonus to saving throws for 1 round.

### Demonic
A sinful corruption gives you divine spells.
- **Spell List:** Divine
- **Bloodline Skills:** Intimidation, Religion
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: acid splash; 1st: fear; 2nd: enlarge; 3rd: slow; 4th: divine wrath; 5th: Abyssal plague; 6th: disintegrate; 7th: divine decree; 8th: divine aura; 9th: implosion
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: demonblood; advanced: demonic form; greater: abyssal rage
- **Blood Magic:** Spiteful curses punish your foes. The first creature that deals damage to you before the end of your next turn takes 2 mental damage per spell level and must attempt a basic Will save.

### Diabolic
A bond with devils gives you divine spells.
- **Spell List:** Divine
- **Bloodline Skills:** Diplomacy, Intimidation
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: detect magic; 1st: command; 2nd: hold person; 3rd: bestow curse; 4th: confusion; 5th: dominate; 6th: true seeing; 7th: project image; 8th: maze; 9th: gate
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: infernal halo; advanced: diabolic form; greater: hellfire storm
- **Blood Magic:** Infernal presence intimidates foes. You or one target gains a +1 status bonus to Intimidation checks for 1 round, or a foe becomes frightened 1.

### Draconic
The blood of dragons grants you arcane spells.
- **Spell List:** Arcane
- **Bloodline Skills:** Arcana, Nature
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: produce flame*; 1st: burning hands*; 2nd: resist energy; 3rd: fireball*; 4th: freedom of movement; 5th: elemental form; 6th: repulsion; 7th: energy aegis; 8th: prismatic wall; 9th: storm of vengeance
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: dragon breath*; advanced: draconic form; greater: dragon wings
- **Dragon Type:** At 1st level, choose the type of dragon that influenced your bloodline. Metallic dragons (brass-fire, bronze-electricity, copper-acid, gold-fire, silver-cold). Chromatic dragons (black-acid, blue-electricity, green-poison, red-fire, white-cold).
- **Blood Magic:** Draconic energy surrounds you or a target. Either you gain a +1 status bonus to AC for 1 round, or a target takes 1 damage per spell level of your dragon type.

### Elemental
A genie ancestor or some other elemental influence has imbued your blood with primal fury.
- **Spell List:** Primal
- **Bloodline Skills:** Intimidation, Nature
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: produce flame*; 1st: burning hands*; 2nd: resist energy; 3rd: fireball*; 4th: freedom of movement; 5th: elemental form; 6th: repulsion; 7th: energy aegis; 8th: prismatic wall; 9th: storm of vengeance
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: elemental toss*; advanced: elemental motion; greater: elemental blast*
- **Elemental Type:** At 1st level, choose the type of elemental: air, earth, fire, or water. For fire, all marked spells deal fire damage. For other elements, they deal bludgeoning damage. You also add the trait of the element you chose.
- **Blood Magic:** Elemental energy surrounds you or a target. Either you gain a +1 status bonus to Intimidation checks for 1 round, or a target takes 1 damage per spell level (bludgeoning or fire according to your elemental type).

### Fey
Influence from the fey gives you primal spells.
- **Spell List:** Primal
- **Bloodline Skills:** Deception, Nature
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: ghost sound; 1st: charm; 2nd: hideous laughter; 3rd: enthrall; 4th: suggestion; 5th: cloak of colors; 6th: mislead; 7th: visions of danger; 8th: uncontrollable dance; 9th: resplendent mansion
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: faerie dust; advanced: fey disappearance; greater: fey glamour
- **Blood Magic:** Colorful fey glamours dance around you or one target, causing them to be concealed for 1 round. Such obvious concealment can't be used to Hide.

### Hag
A hag long ago cursed your family, or you are a descendant of a hag or changeling, and their accursed corruption infests your blood and soul.
- **Spell List:** Occult
- **Bloodline Skills:** Deception, Occultism
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: daze; 1st: illusory disguise; 2nd: touch of idiocy; 3rd: blindness; 4th: outcast's curse; 5th: mariner's curse; 6th: baleful polymorph; 7th: warp mind; 8th: spiritual epidemic; 9th: nature's enmity
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: jealous hex; advanced: horrific visage; greater: you're mine
- **Blood Magic:** Hag's curse marks a foe. The first creature that deals damage to you before the end of your next turn takes 2 negative damage per spell level and must attempt a basic Fortitude save or be slowed 1 for 1 round.

### Imperial
One of your ancestors was a mortal who mastered magic. Such magical blood can remain latent for generations, but in you it manifested in full.
- **Spell List:** Arcane
- **Bloodline Skills:** Arcana, Society
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: detect magic; 1st: magic missile; 2nd: dispel magic; 3rd: haste; 4th: dimension door; 5th: prying eye; 6th: disintegrate; 7th: prismatic spray; 8th: maze; 9th: prismatic sphere
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: ancestral memories; advanced: extend spell; greater: arcane countermeasure
- **Blood Magic:** A surge of ancestral memories grants you or one target a +1 status bonus to skill checks for 1 round.

### Undead
The touch of undeath runs through your blood. Your family tree might contain powerful undead, like a vampire, or perhaps you died and returned a bit different.
- **Spell List:** Divine
- **Bloodline Skills:** Intimidation, Religion
- **Granted Spells:** cantrip: chill touch; 1st: harm; 2nd: false life; 3rd: bind undead; 4th: talking corpse; 5th: cloudkill; 6th: vampiric exsanguination; 7th: finger of death; 8th: horrid wilting; 9th: wail of the banshee
- **Bloodline Spells:** initial: touch of undeath; advanced: drain life; greater: grasping grave
- **Blood Magic:** Necromantic energy flows through you or one target. Either you gain temporary Hit Points equal to the spell's level for 1 round, or a target takes 1 negative damage per spell level.

## Sorcerer Feats by Level

### 1st Level Feats
- **Counterspell (Reaction):** Trigger a creature casts a spell you have in your repertoire and you can see its manifestations. Expend one of your spell slots to counteract the triggering spell. You lose your spell slot as if you had cast the triggering spell, then attempt to counteract it. Has the trait corresponding to your tradition.
- **Dangerous Sorcery:** When you Cast a Spell from your spell slots that deals damage and doesn't have a duration, gain a status bonus to that spell's damage equal to the spell's level.
- **Familiar:** An animal serves you and assists your spellcasting. You gain a familiar.
- **Reach Spell (Metamagic):** If the next action you use is to Cast a Spell that has a range, increase that spell's range by 30 feet. If the spell normally has a range of touch, extend it to 30 feet.
- **Widen Spell (Metamagic):** If the next action you use is to Cast a Spell with an area (burst, cone, or line) without duration, increase its area: +5 feet radius for bursts with at least 10-foot radius; +5 feet length for cones/lines 15 feet or smaller; +10 feet for larger cones/lines.

### 2nd Level Feats
- **Cantrip Expansion:** Add two additional cantrips from your spell list to your repertoire.
- **Enhanced Familiar (Prereq: familiar):** You can select four familiar or master abilities each day instead of two.

### 4th Level Feats
- **Arcane Evolution (Arcane Sorcerer):** Become trained in one skill of your choice. Keep a book of arcane spells similar to a wizard's spellbook. Add all spells in your repertoire to this book for free, and can add additional arcane spells by paying cost and using Arcana. During daily preparations, choose any spell from your book; if not in your repertoire, add it until next preparation. If already in repertoire, add as additional signature spell for that day.
- **Bespell Weapon (Free Action):** Requirements: most recent action was to cast a non-cantrip spell. Siphon residual energy into one weapon you're wielding. Until end of turn, weapon deals extra 1d6 damage depending on school: Abjuration-force; Conjuration/Transmutation-same as weapon; Divination/Enchantment/Illusion-mental; Evocation-type spell dealt or force if none; Necromancy-negative.
- **Divine Evolution (Divine Sorcerer):** Gain an additional spell slot of your highest level, usable only to cast heal or harm. Can cast either using that slot even if not in repertoire.
- **Occult Evolution (Occult Sorcerer):** Become trained in one skill of your choice. Once per day, spend 1 minute to choose one mental occult spell you don't know and add it to your repertoire. Lose this temporary spell next daily preparations.
- **Primal Evolution (Primal Sorcerer):** Gain an additional spell slot of your highest level, usable only to cast summon animal or summon plants and fungi. Can cast either using that slot even if not in repertoire.

### 6th Level Feats
- **Advanced Bloodline:** Gain the advanced bloodline spell associated with your bloodline. Increase focus pool by 1 Focus Point.
- **Steady Spellcasting:** If a reaction would disrupt your spellcasting action, attempt DC 15 flat check. On success, your action isn't disrupted.

### 8th Level Feats
- **Bloodline Resistance:** Gain a +1 status bonus to saving throws against spells and magical effects.
- **Crossblooded Evolution:** Have one spell in your repertoire from a tradition other than the one matching your bloodline. Cast that spell as a spell from your bloodline's tradition. Can swap which spell/tradition, but can't have more than one spell from another tradition at same time.

### 10th Level Feats
- **Greater Bloodline:** Gain the greater bloodline spell associated with your bloodline. Increase focus pool by 1 Focus Point.
- **Overwhelming Energy (Metamagic):** If next action is to Cast a Spell, the spell ignores an amount of target's resistance to acid, cold, electricity, fire, or sonic damage equal to your level. Applies to all damage including persistent and ongoing effects. Immunities unaffected.
- **Quickened Casting (Free Action) (Metamagic):** Frequency once per day. If next action is to cast a sorcerer cantrip or sorcerer spell at least 2 levels lower than highest level you can cast, reduce number of actions by 1 (minimum 1 action).

### 12th Level Feats
- **Bloodline Focus:** If you have spent at least 2 Focus Points since last Refocus, recover 2 Focus Points when you Refocus instead of 1.
- **Magic Sense (Detection):** You can sense presence of magic auras as though always using 1st-level detect magic in your field of vision. When you Seek, gain benefits of 3rd-level detect magic on things you see. Can turn this sense off/on with free action at start/end of turn.

### 14th Level Feats
- **Interweave Dispel (Metamagic):** Prereq: dispel magic in repertoire. If next action is to cast single-target spell against creature, and you hit with attack roll or foe fails save, can cast dispel magic on foe as free action, expending spell slot normally, targeting one spell effect affecting the foe.
- **Reflect Spell:** When successfully using Counterspell to counteract a spell affecting targeted creatures or area, turn that spell's effect back on its caster. Reflected spell affects only original caster even if area spell or would affect more than one creature. Original caster can attempt save and use other defenses as normal.

### 16th Level Feats
- **Effortless Concentration (Reaction/Free Action):** Trigger: your turn begins. You immediately gain effects of Sustain a Spell action, extending duration of one active sorcerer spell.
- **Greater Mental Evolution (Arcane/Occult Sorcerer):** Add one spell to your repertoire for each spell level you can cast.
- **Greater Vital Evolution (Divine/Primal Sorcerer):** Twice per day, cast a spell after running out of spell slots of appropriate level; the two spells must be of different spell levels.

### 18th Level Feats
- **Bloodline Wellspring:** If you have spent at least 3 Focus Points since last Refocus, recover 3 Focus Points when you Refocus instead of 1.
- **Greater Crossblooded Evolution:** Have up to three spells from other traditions in your repertoire instead of just one. These must each be of different spell levels but don't need to be from same tradition.

### 20th Level Feats
- **Bloodline Conduit (Metamagic):** Frequency once per minute. If next action is to Cast a Spell of 5th level or lower with no duration, you don't expend the spell's slot when you cast it.
- **Bloodline Perfection:** Gain an additional 10th-level spell slot.
- **Metamagic Mastery:** Altering your spells doesn't take any longer than casting them normally. You can use metamagic single actions as free actions.
