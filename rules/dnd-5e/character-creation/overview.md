# Character Creation Overview

Here are the steps to make a character; each step is explored in detail below:

1. **Choose a Class.** Every adventurer is a member of a class. A class broadly describes a character's vocation, special talents, and favored tactics.

2. **Determine Your Origin.** An origin includes two elements: background and species. How did the character spend the years leading up to a life of adventure? What was their family like? You can also choose your character's languages.

3. **Determine Ability Scores.** A character's six abilities determine much of what they can do in the game.

4. **Choose an Alignment.** Alignment is a shorthand for your character's moral compass.

5. **Fill in Details.** Using the choices you've made, fill in the remaining details on your character sheet.

## Choose a Character Sheet

You'll record the main details of your character on a character sheet. Throughout this chapter, we use the term "character sheet" to mean whatever you use to track your character's details, whether it's a printed character sheet, a digital character sheet, or plain paper. Choose whichever style of sheet works for you, and then embark on creating a character!

## Write Your Level

Write your character's level on your character sheet. Typically, a character starts at level 1 and advances in level by adventuring and gaining Experience Points (XP).

### Starting XP

- A level 1 character has 0 XP
- If the GM starts you at a higher level, write your chosen subclass on your character sheet if you start at level 3 or higher
- You begin with the minimum amount of XP required to reach your starting level

## Write Your XP

Record your Experience Points. As you adventure and gain XP, update this number. When your XP total equals or exceeds a number in the Character Advancement table, you reach the corresponding level.
