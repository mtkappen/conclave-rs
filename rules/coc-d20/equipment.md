### Weapon Properties

- **Light**: Can be used in off-hand with -4 penalty (or -2 with Two-Weapon Fighting feat)
- **One-handed**: Standard melee weapon
- **Two-handed**: Requires two hands; adds 1.5× Strength to damage
- **Reach**: Doubles your reach for melee attacks
- **Trip**: Can attempt trip attack without provoking opportunity attack

## Expanded Firearms Rules

### Automatic Fire

Fully automatic weapons can fire multiple rounds in a single attack:

| Burst Size    | Attack Penalty | Damage Bonus      |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2-round       | -2             | +1d6              |
| 3-round       | -4             | +2d6              |
| Full auto     | -6             | +3d6 (spray)      |

### Spray Attacks

Full automatic fire creates a spray pattern:
- Affects all targets in a 5-foot cone
- Each target makes separate attack roll against AC
- Uses 10+ rounds of ammunition

## Firearms and the Law

Firearm laws vary significantly by era. Investigators must be aware of legal restrictions.

### National Firearms Act of 1934 (NFA)

Regulates specific firearms:
- Machine guns
- Short-barreled rifles/shotguns
- Silencers
- Destructive devices

**Requirements:**
- $200 tax stamp per item (equivalent to ~$3,500 today)
- Background check
- Registration with ATF

### Gun Control Act of 1968

Major federal firearms legislation:
- Prohibits interstate firearm sales (except through FFL)
- Bans mail-order sales of rifles/shotguns
- Prohibits sales to felons, mental patients, drug users
- Requires licensed dealers for all sales

### Brady Handgun Control Act (1993)

Implemented waiting periods and background checks:
- 5-day waiting period (initially)
- NICS background check system
- Still in effect today
