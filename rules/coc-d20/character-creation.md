# Chapter 1: Character Creation

Creating a character in Call of Cthulhu d20 involves determining your ability scores, choosing a profession template, selecting skills, and fleshing out your character's background and personality.

## Your Ability Scores

Your character has six abilities:
- **Strength (STR)** - Muscle and physical power
- **Dexterity (DEX)** - Hand-eye coordination, agility, reflexes, and balance
- **Constitution (CON)** - Health and stamina
- **Intelligence (INT)** - Learning, reasoning, and skill points
- **Wisdom (WIS)** - Willpower, common sense, perception, and intuition
- **Charisma (CHA)** - Force of personality, persuasiveness, and leadership

### Generating Ability Scores

To create an ability score for your character:
1. Roll four six-sided dice (4d6)
2. Disregard the lowest die
3. Total the three highest dice
4. This gives you a number between 3 (terrible) and 18 (excellent)

Make this roll six times, recording each result. Once you have all six scores, assign each score to one of your character's six abilities.

### Ability Modifiers

| Score | Modifier |
|-------|----------|
| 1     | -5       |
| 2-3   | -4       |
| 4-5   | -3       |
| 6-7   | -2       |
| 8-9   | -1       |
| 10-11 | +0       |
| 12-13 | +1       |
| 14-15 | +2       |
| 16-17 | +3       |
| 18-19 | +4       |
| 20-21 | +5       |

### Rerolling Option

If your scores are too low, you may scrap them and roll all six scores over. Your scores are considered too low if:
- Your total modifiers add up to 0 or less, OR
- Your highest score is 13 or lower

## Strength (STR)

Strength measures your character's muscle and physical power. It's most important to those intending to get into hand-to-hand combat.

**You apply your Strength modifier to:**
- Melee attack rolls
- Damage rolls when using a melee weapon or thrown weapon
- Climb, Jump, and Swim checks (these skills have Strength as their key ability)
- Strength checks (for situations such as breaking down doors)

## Dexterity (DEX)

Dexterity measures your character's hand-eye coordination, agility, reflexes, and balance. If you want your character to be a marksman or good at avoiding damage, put a high score in Dexterity.

**You apply your Dexterity modifier to:**
- Ranged attack rolls, including attacks made with guns, bows, throwing knives, and other ranged weapons
- Your Armor Class score (provided your character can react to the attack)
- Reflex saving throws (for avoiding explosions and other attacks that you can escape by moving quickly)
- Balance, Drive, Escape Artist, Hide, Move Silently, Open Lock, Operate Heavy Machinery, Pilot, Ride, Sleight of Hand, Tumble, and Use Rope checks

## Constitution (CON)

Constitution represents your character's health and stamina. Since a good Constitution improves hit points, it's important to everyone.

**You apply your Constitution modifier to:**
- Each Hit Die roll (though a penalty can never drop a Hit Die roll below 1)
- Fortitude saving throws (for resisting poison and similar threats)
- Concentration checks (this skill has Constitution as its key ability)

Constitution also determines how long a character can hold her breath (for example, when trying to avoid drowning).

If a character's Constitution changes enough to alter his Constitution modifier, his hit points also increase or decrease accordingly. This applies even to Hit Dice gained from previous levels.

## Intelligence (INT)

Intelligence describes how well your character learns and reasons. If you want your character to have a lot of skills and be smart, put a high score in Intelligence.

**You apply your Intelligence modifier to:**
- The number of languages your character knows at the start of the game
- The number of skill points gained each level (minimum 1 skill point per level)
- Appraise, Computer Use, Craft, Demolitions, Disable Device, Forgery, Knowledge, Open Lock, Operate Heavy Machinery, Repair, Research, Search, Speak Other Language, and Spellcraft checks

## Wisdom (WIS)

Wisdom describes a character's willpower, common sense, perception, and intuition. While Intelligence represents one's ability to analyze information, Wisdom is more a case of being in tune with and aware of one's surroundings. An "absentminded professor" has low Wisdom and high Intelligence. A holy hermit might be unsophisticated (low Intelligence) yet still have great insight (high Wisdom).

**You apply your Wisdom modifier to:**
- Will saving throws (for negating mind-affecting spells)
- Heal, Innuendo, Listen, Psychic Focus, Psychoanalysis, Sense Motive, Spot, and Wilderness Lore checks

Wisdom also determines a character's starting Sanity.

## Charisma (CHA)

Charisma measures a character's force of personality, persuasiveness, personal magnetism, leadership ability, and physical attractiveness. It represents actual inner strength or strength of personality, not merely how one is perceived by others in a given social setting.

**You apply your Charisma modifier to:**
- Animal Empathy, Bluff, Diplomacy, Disguise, Gather Information, Handle Animal, Intimidate, and Performance checks

## Changing Ability Scores

Over time, the ability scores your character starts with can change:

1. **Level Increases**: Add 1 point to any one score at 4th level and every four levels thereafter (at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 20th level)
2. **Spells and Magical Effects**: Many spells temporarily increase or decrease ability scores
3. **Ability Damage**: Poisons, diseases, and other effects can harm an ability temporarily. Ability points lost to damage return on their own at a rate of 1 point per day for each damaged ability
4. **Ability Drain**: Certain horrible creatures drain abilities, resulting in permanent loss. In Call of Cthulhu, no spells can restore this loss
5. **Aging**: As a character ages, some ability scores go up and others go down

When an ability score changes permanently, all attributes associated with that score change accordingly.

## Characters and Levels

An attack roll, saving throw, or skill check is a combination of three numbers:
1. A random factor (the number you roll on 1d20)
2. A number representing the character's innate abilities (the ability modifier)
3. A bonus representing the character's experience and training

### Defense Option vs Offense Option

When you create a character, you either choose the **Defense Option** or the **Offense Option**:

- **Defense Option**: Provides for better saving throws, but a poorer attack bonus
- **Offense Option**: Offers a better attack bonus, but poorer saving throws

#### Base Save Bonus Progressions

Each character gets three save progressions to assign to Fortitude, Reflex, and Will:

**Defense Option:**
| Level | Good Save | Good Save | Bad Save | BAB |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|-----|
| 1st   | +2        | +2        | +0       | +0  |
| 2nd   | +3        | +3        | +0       | +1  |
| 3rd   | +3        | +3        | +1       | +1  |

**Offense Option:**
| Level | Good Save | Bad Save | Bad Save | BAB |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------|-----|
| 1st   | +2        | +0       | +0       | +1  |
| 2nd   | +3        | +0       | +0       | +2  |
| 3rd   | +3        | +1       | +1       | +3  |

### Level-Dependent Benefits

Characters gain various benefits as they advance in level:

- **Feats**: Each character starts with two feats at 1st level and gains another feat at every level divisible by three (3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th, 15th, and 18th)
- **Ability Increases**: Upon gaining any level divisible by four (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 20th), a character increases one of her ability scores by 1 point
- **Hit Points**: Each character begins with 6 hit points, modified by Constitution. Each time a character gains a new level, roll 1d6 (a Hit Die), add or subtract Constitution modifier, then add to hit point total

## Choosing Core Skills

Every character gets twelve skills from the skill list as core skills. These are the skills acquired most easily by that character—they have a cost of one skill point per rank. All other skills cost two skill points per rank.

Each rank gives +1 to a skill check. The maximum rank in a core skill is your level + 3. The maximum rank in a noncore skill is half of this number.

### Skill Points at 1st Level

At 1st level, you get (8 + Intelligence modifier) × 4 skill points.

### Skill Points at Higher Levels

At each subsequent level, you get 8 + Intelligence modifier skill points (minimum 1).

## Variant: Defense Bonus

In Lovecraft's stories, investigators are often ordinary people, and powerful creatures can dispatch them quite readily. If your Gamemaster prefers a campaign that feels a little more like "pulp adventure," particularly one that features human opponents more than creatures, this variant rule may be for you.

In this variant, each character has a "defense bonus" that can be added to her Armor Class. Only investigators get this bonus; ordinary people do not. A character's defense bonus increases every five levels.

| Level | Defense Option | Offense Option |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1st   | +2             | +0             |
| 2nd   | +2             | +0             |
| 3rd   | +2             | +0             |
| 4th   | +2             | +0             |
| 5th   | +3             | +1             |
| 6th   | +3             | +1             |
| 7th   | +3             | +1             |
| 8th   | +3             | +1             |
| 9th   | +3             | +1             |
| 10th  | +4             | +2             |

To balance the two options, an Offense Option character also receives an extra Weapon Proficiency feat at 1st level.
