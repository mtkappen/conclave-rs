# Deception (Charisma)

Deception measures your ability to lie, trick, and mislead others.

## Untrained Actions

### Create a Diversion [one-action]
You attempt to create a distraction so you can hide. Make a Deception check against the Perception DC of creatures in the area. On a success, you become concealed from those creatures for 1 round, allowing you to use the Hide action even if you were previously observed.

- **Critical Success:** You create an effective diversion; all creatures in the area are distracted for 1 round and you gain a +2 circumstance bonus to Stealth checks to hide during that time.
- **Success:** You create a distraction; creatures in the area are distracted for 1 round, allowing you to Hide.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; creatures notice what you're doing.
- **Critical Failure:** Your attempt draws unwanted attention; creatures become suspicious of your actions.

### Feint [one-action] - DECEPTION
**Requirements:** You can see and communicate with the target.

You attempt to fool a creature into lowering its defenses, making it flat-footed against your next attack. Make a Deception check against the target's Will DC. If you have a specific attack in mind, you must declare it before rolling; if you don't specify an attack, the target is flat-footed against any of your attacks until the end of your next turn.

- **Critical Success:** The target is flat-footed to all your attacks until the end of your next turn.
- **Success:** The target is flat-footed to your next attack against it before the end of your next turn.
- **Failure:** Your attempt fails; the target remains unimpressed.
- **Critical Failure:** The target becomes aware of your attempt and gains a +2 circumstance bonus to AC against your attacks for 1 round.

## Trained Actions

### Impersonate - EXPLORATION
You attempt to disguise yourself as someone else. This requires appropriate costume or disguise materials and takes at least 10 minutes to prepare. Make a Deception check against the Perception DC of creatures who might interact with you. The GM may adjust the DC based on how well you know the person you're impersonating and how closely you'll be observed.

- **Critical Success:** Your disguise is convincing even under close scrutiny; observers believe you are the person you're impersonating.
- **Success:** Your disguise passes casual inspection but might fail under detailed examination or if someone knows the real person well.
- **Failure:** Observers suspect something is amiss with your appearance or behavior.
- **Critical Failure:** Your disguise is obviously fake; observers immediately recognize you as an impostor.

### Lie - CONCENTRATE
You attempt to convince a target of something false. The GM determines the DC based on how believable the lie is and the target's disposition toward you. Some lies might be impossible to believe regardless of your skill.

- **Critical Success:** The target believes your lie completely and may act accordingly.
- **Success:** The target believes your lie, at least temporarily.
- **Failure:** The target doesn't believe your lie.
- **Critical Failure:** The target not only disbelieves your lie but becomes suspicious of your motives.

**Lie DC Examples:**
| Lie Type | Typical DC |
|----------|------------|
| Simple white lie | 10-15 |
| Plausible fabrication | 15-20 |
| Complex deception | 20-25 |
| Outrageous claim | 25+ |
